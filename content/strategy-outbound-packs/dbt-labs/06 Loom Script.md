# dbt Labs - Loom Script

## Working Title
dbt Labs AI Visibility & Content Infrastructure Gap — Full Breakdown

## Opener
I want to walk you through something we found when we mapped the data transformation category for AI search and organic visibility.

dbt Labs is the most well-known transformation tool in modern data. Strong brand. Massive community. Over 5,700 referring domains — more than almost any competitor in the space.

And yet, across the three surfaces where B2B buyers increasingly start their tool evaluations — ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews — dbt is nearly invisible.

I'm going to show you exactly what we found, why it's happening, and what the opportunity looks like if dbt closes the gap.

## Breakdown
First, the AI visibility data. We tested 16 real buyer prompts across three platforms. These are the kinds of queries data engineering leads and VPs of Data are asking when they start evaluating tools.

ChatGPT: dbt was mentioned zero times out of sixteen prompts. For one prompt — 'best data transformation tools' — ChatGPT didn't even interpret it as a tooling question. It returned statistical techniques. No vendors at all.

Gemini: dbt was mentioned once out of sixteen. As a source link — not as the recommended tool. Coalesce.io was the primary cited source.

Google AI Overviews: dbt appeared twice out of sixteen. But here's the important part — the source citations pointed to third-party listicle pages by Datameer, Astera, and Coalesce. Not to anything dbt Labs owns.

Now here's the part that makes this structural instead of random. Coalesce has approximately 526 referring domains. dbt has 5,714. That's roughly a ten-to-one authority advantage. But Coalesce gets cited more often because it has the decision-stage pages AI models pull from — buyer guides, comparison pages, structured tool evaluations.

Second, the organic search landscape. The data transformation category generates 11.7 million monthly searches. Almost 98% of that demand is unbranded — buyers looking for a solution, not a specific vendor.

The buyer-guide cluster alone — 'best ETL tools,' 'data transformation tools,' 'extract transform load tools' — represents 1.9 million monthly searches. dbt Labs has zero owned pages targeting any of them.

Add in bottom-of-funnel comparison queries — 'ETL vs ELT,' 'dbt vs Matillion,' 'data lake vs data warehouse' — and that's another 21,190 monthly searches. Also uncaptured.

dbt currently receives about 111,508 organic monthly visits. Against an 11.7 million search opportunity, that's roughly 1% capture.

Third, why this is happening. dbt's content is overwhelmingly developer documentation and community content. That's excellent for adoption — it's why dbt has the community it has. But it doesn't serve the decision stage. AI models and search engines need structured decision-stage content to recommend a tool: buyer guides, side-by-side comparisons, evaluation frameworks.

dbt's authority is pointed at the wrong content layer for buying consideration. The authority is there. The pages aren't.

## First Move
So what does the fix look like? The modeled base case shows 419,200 monthly visits within 12 months if dbt builds decision-stage pages against the highest-intent query clusters. That's an estimate — actual results depend on execution and competitive dynamics.

The first 6-month target focuses on 155,104 monthly visits — concentrated on buyer-guide and comparison queries where purchase intent is highest.

On the AI visibility side, building the structured decision-stage pages AI models need is the foundational step. Paired with dbt's existing authority base, those pages are positioned to earn citations faster than any competitor starting from scratch.

I want to be clear about what I can and can't model. Traffic projections are estimates based on current demand and competitive positioning. Revenue impact requires dbt's actual ACV, AOV, and funnel conversion rates — that's first-party data I don't have. But the structural gap is clear: missing decision-stage pages on 1.9 million buyer-guide searches is a pipeline leak, not a branding problem.

## Close
The bottom line: dbt Labs has the strongest authority foundation in data transformation. It has the brand. It has the community. What it doesn't have is the content infrastructure to convert that authority into buyer visibility on the surfaces where purchasing decisions now start.

The window to close this gap is narrowing. Competitors are building the pages AI models reference. Every month without decision-stage content is another month of buying consideration routed elsewhere.
