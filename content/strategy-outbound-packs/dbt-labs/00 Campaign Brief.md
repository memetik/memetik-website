# dbt Labs - Campaign Brief

## Core Thesis
dbt Labs is the most recognized transformation tool in modern data — and it is almost invisible on the AI-powered search surfaces where buyers now start their evaluations. The gap is not brand awareness; it is content infrastructure. dbt Labs lacks the decision-stage pages that AI models need to recommend it, and competitors with a fraction of its authority are getting cited instead.

## Why This Pack Exists
Over 60% of B2B buyers now begin tool research inside AI-powered search surfaces. Across 16 tested buyer prompts, dbt Labs has a 0% ChatGPT mention rate and 6% Gemini mention rate. Meanwhile, Coalesce.io — with roughly one-tenth the referring-domain authority — is being cited in AI answers for queries dbt should own. Every month this gap stays open, buying consideration flows to weaker competitors with stronger content positioning. The 11.7M-monthly-search category is unowned and the window to claim it is narrowing as competitors invest.

## Approved Proof Points
- 11.7M monthly searches in the data transformation category (validated demand).
- 0% ChatGPT mention rate across 16 tested buyer prompts.
- 6.3% Gemini mention rate (1 out of 16 prompts).
- 12.5% Google AI Overview mention rate (2 out of 16 prompts).
- 97.7% of category demand is unbranded — buyers searching for solutions, not specific vendors.
- 1.9M monthly searches in buyer-guide queries alone with no owned dbt Labs pages targeting them.
- dbt Labs has 5,714 referring domains and 67,348 backlinks — a stronger authority base than every direct competitor except Gartner.
- Coalesce.io has approximately 526 referring domains (roughly 1/10th of dbt's authority) yet gets cited in AI answers for 'best data transformation tools.'
- 419,200 modeled monthly visits achievable within 12 months (estimate-only, base case).
- Aggressive upside modeled at 666,904 monthly visits (estimate-only).
- First 6-month target: 155,104 monthly visits (estimate-only).
- 21,190 monthly searches in the alternatives and comparisons cluster (ETL vs ELT, dbt vs Matillion, data lake vs data warehouse).
- dbt was mentioned in a Google AI Overview for 'best data transformation tools' but the source citations pointed to Datameer, Astera, and Coalesce listicle pages — not dbt Labs owned content.
- Gemini cited getdbt.com once as a source link in a 'data transformation comparison' answer, but Coalesce.io was the primary cited source.

## Claims To Avoid
- Do not state revenue projections — revenue planning requires first-party ACV/AOV and funnel inputs.
- Do not present traffic projections as guaranteed; always use 'modeled,' 'estimate-only,' or 'projected.'
- Do not claim dbt is completely absent from all AI surfaces — it does appear in some Google AI Overviews and one Gemini response.
- Do not invent Perplexity data — that platform probe failed and returned no usable results.
- Do not claim certainty about ChatGPT results for all prompts — one prompt timed out and returned no response.
- Do not use internal system labels in any founder-facing copy.
- Do not invent competitor metrics, claims, or data points not present in the approved brief.
- Do not imply revenue certainty or ROI guarantees without first-party funnel data.

## Angle Map
### Traffic
dbt Labs sits on 11.7M monthly searches of category demand and captures a fraction of it. 97.7% of that demand is unbranded — buyers looking for a solution, not a vendor. The buyer-guide cluster alone represents 1.9M monthly searches with zero owned dbt Labs pages targeting them. This is the largest unclaimed traffic opportunity in data transformation.

### Revenue
dbt Labs has the category authority and brand recognition to convert the largest buyer-intent search clusters in data transformation — but without the decision-stage pages that capture purchase-ready demand, that potential revenue never enters the funnel. Revenue planning requires first-party ACV/AOV and funnel inputs, but the structural gap is clear: 1.9M monthly buyer-guide searches and 21,190 monthly comparison searches with zero owned pages is a pipeline leak, not a brand problem.

### AI Visibility
dbt Labs has a 0% ChatGPT mention rate, a 6% Gemini mention rate, and a 12.5% Google AI Overview mention rate across 16 tested buyer prompts. Meanwhile, Coalesce.io — with roughly one-tenth the referring-domain authority — is getting cited in AI answers for the exact queries dbt should dominate. This isn't a brand problem; it's a content infrastructure problem. AI models can only recommend tools they can find structured decision-stage content for, and dbt Labs hasn't built that content.
