# dbt Labs - Revenue Angle

## Core Angle
dbt Labs has the category authority and brand recognition to convert the largest buyer-intent search clusters in data transformation — but without the decision-stage pages that capture purchase-ready demand, that potential revenue never enters the funnel. Revenue planning requires first-party ACV/AOV and funnel inputs, but the structural gap is clear: 1.9M monthly buyer-guide searches and 21,190 monthly comparison searches with zero owned pages is a pipeline leak, not a brand problem.

## Why It Matters Now
The data transformation buying process is shifting to AI-powered surfaces where recommendation drives shortlist inclusion. dbt Labs is absent from these surfaces at the exact moment when the category is experiencing its highest-ever search volume. Competitors with weaker products are capturing the buying consideration that should flow through dbt's funnel. The cost of inaction is compounding: every month without decision-stage pages is another month of buying consideration routed to competitors.

## Approved Proof Points
- 1.9M monthly searches in buyer-guide queries — 'best ETL tools,' 'data transformation tools' — with no owned dbt Labs pages. These are the queries where buyers form vendor preferences and enter sales funnels.
- 21,190 monthly searches in pure comparison and alternatives queries — 'ETL vs ELT,' 'dbt vs Matillion,' 'data lake vs data warehouse.' These are bottom-of-funnel evaluation queries.
- 97.7% of category demand is unbranded — buyers who haven't chosen a vendor yet. This is addressable pipeline.
- Coalesce.io, with roughly 1/10th the authority, is getting cited in AI answers for buyer-intent queries dbt should own — meaning buying consideration is actively flowing to competitors.
- 419,200 modeled monthly visits within 12 months (estimate-only, base case). Revenue impact depends on dbt Labs' actual ACV/AOV and funnel conversion rates.
- dbt Labs' existing 5,714 referring domains mean new decision-stage pages can rank faster than a competitor starting from scratch — the authority investment is already made.

## Claims To Avoid
- Do not project revenue numbers — revenue planning requires first-party ACV/AOV and funnel inputs from dbt Labs.
- Do not claim a specific pipeline dollar value without client funnel data.
- Do not guarantee conversion rates or lead volumes.
- Do not imply that traffic automatically equals revenue — the connection depends on page design, funnel architecture, and sales process.
- Do not present the revenue angle as a replacement for dbt Labs' existing demand generation — this is additive infrastructure.
