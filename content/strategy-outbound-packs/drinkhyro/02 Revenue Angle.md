# Hyro - Revenue Angle

## Core Angle
Hyro's commercial gap is shortlist access. The brand needs to show up where buyers compare options, not just where people browse the category.

## Why It Matters Now
The best early commercial queries are already mapped, Hyro has visible gaps on high-intent modifiers, and the right decision pages can increase qualified discovery before shoppers lock into incumbents.

## Approved Proof Points
- Buyer Guides demand is 211,170, Alternatives & Comparisons demand is 7,210, and Reviews & Social Proof demand is 3,720.
- Commercial keyword signals already exist around best sugar free electrolyte drink, best sugar-free electrolyte drink, best low-sugar electrolyte drink, best no sugar electrolyte powder, best cheap electrolyte powder, and best electrolyte drink without potassium.
- Hyro's first pages should focus on buyer guides, then expand into comparisons, reviews, and use-case coverage.
- Modeled traffic upside suggests more qualified product discovery, but revenue planning still requires first-party ACV/AOV and funnel inputs.
- The goal is earlier shortlist inclusion before buyers default to LMNT, Liquid I.V., Nuun, or DripDrop.

## Claims To Avoid
- Do not turn traffic estimates into revenue projections.
- Do not promise conversion lift without first-party funnel data.
- Do not position the strategy as only competitor capture; the bigger prize is unbranded decision demand.
- Do not claim certainty on unit economics, CAC, or payback.

A lot of brands chase more traffic and hope revenue follows.

But the smarter move is to ask where buyers actually decide.

That means the commercial opportunity is not abstract.

It is tied to pages that help buyers compare options and enter a shortlist.

The first move is to ship those decision pages, then support them with comparison content, reviews, and outside proof.

That can improve qualified discovery, but I would not pretend we have revenue certainty from this alone.

Revenue planning still requires first-party ACV, AOV, and funnel inputs.
