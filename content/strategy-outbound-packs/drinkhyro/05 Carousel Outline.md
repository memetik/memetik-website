# Hyro - Carousel Outline

## Traffic

### Slide 01 — Hyro is missing the moment buyers choose
The fastest growth move is not more generic hydration content. It is owning the decision-stage searches where shoppers decide which electrolyte product to trust.

### Slide 02 — The category is much bigger than current visibility
Hyro currently has 1,004 organic visits and 362 organic keywords. The validated search opportunity is 8,464,050.

### Slide 03 — The best wedge is buyer guides
Buyer Guides demand is 211,170, with clear terms like best electrolyte drink, best electrolyte powder, best hydration powder, best electrolyte mix, and best electrolyte drink mix.

### Slide 04 — This is mostly open-market demand
92.6% of the validated opportunity is non-competitor and unbranded. The prize is not only taking traffic from incumbents. It is showing up before a buyer chooses any brand.

### Slide 05 — Hyro has enough authority to open the wedge
With 74 referring domains and 366 backlinks, Hyro already has enough authority to support a focused first move around decision-stage pages.

### Slide 06 — Model the upside, but keep it honest
Modeled, estimate-only traffic potential is 70,857 in the first 6 months and 191,505 in 12 months, with aggressive upside of 327,134. These are planning estimates, not guarantees.

### Slide 07 — What to ship first
Start with buyer-guide pages, then expand into comparisons and supporting proof pages. Publish with clean indexing, schema, canonicals, and matching reinforcement around the web.

## Revenue

### Slide 01 — Hyro has a shortlist problem
The commercial gap is not category interest. It is missing the pages buyers use to compare options and decide what makes the shortlist.

### Slide 02 — The commercial clusters are already mapped
Buyer Guides demand is 211,170, Alternatives & Comparisons demand is 7,210, and Reviews & Social Proof demand is 3,720.

### Slide 03 — High-intent modifiers are visible now
Commercial signals already exist around terms like best sugar-free electrolyte drink, best low-sugar electrolyte drink, best no sugar electrolyte powder, best cheap electrolyte powder, and best electrolyte drink without potassium.

### Slide 04 — The first move is not broad content volume
Hyro should focus on decision-stage buyer guides first, then expand into comparisons, reviews, and use-case coverage that helps buyers evaluate options.

### Slide 05 — Why this matters commercially
The goal is earlier qualified discovery before shoppers default to LMNT, Liquid I.V., Nuun, or DripDrop.

### Slide 06 — Be clear about the limits
Modeled traffic upside can suggest more product discovery, but revenue planning still requires first-party ACV/AOV and funnel inputs.

### Slide 07 — The real system
Decision pages create entry points. Comparisons and reviews strengthen trust. Supporting proof helps the brand stay visible across search and answer surfaces.

## AI Visibility

### Slide 01 — Hyro is missing answer surfaces in buying moments
This is not just an SEO issue. It is a visibility issue when buyers ask for the best electrolyte option and scan recommendations before clicking.

### Slide 02 — Sampled prompt baseline
In sampled commercial prompts, Hyro had 0/8 mentions in ChatGPT, 0/8 in Gemini, and 0/8 in Google AI Overview.

### Slide 03 — Who is shaping the visibility layer now
The brands appearing in the category conversation include LMNT, Liquid I.V., Nuun, and DripDrop.

### Slide 04 — Pages alone are not enough
Hyro needs the right decision-stage pages, but those pages also need review reinforcement, third-party proof, and clean publishing basics to become easier to cite and compare.

### Slide 05 — The content focus stays the same
Start with buyer-guide and comparison pages where commercial intent is highest, then build supporting proof around those pages so the visibility move compounds.

### Slide 06 — What success looks like
The objective is shortlist inclusion across search and answer surfaces so Hyro becomes easier to find, compare, and mention before shoppers default to incumbents.

### Slide 07 — Use the evidence carefully
The prompt data is sample-based, which makes it useful for prioritization but not a claim about every platform result in every context.
