# Hyro - Loom Script

## Working Title
Hyro's fastest growth move: own decision-stage electrolyte queries and reinforce them with proof

## Opener
If I were prioritizing Hyro's next content move, I would not start with broad awareness content.

I would start where buyers are already deciding which electrolyte product to trust.

That is the fastest path to make Hyro easier to find, compare, and mention before shoppers default to incumbents.

## Breakdown
Right now, Hyro has 1,004 organic visits and 362 organic keywords in a category with 8,464,050 validated searches.

The opening is especially strong because 92.6% of that validated opportunity is non-competitor and unbranded.

That means the prize is not only competitor capture. It is being present before buyers have chosen any brand at all.

The clearest first wedge is buyer guides, where demand alone is 211,170.

The first pages I would prioritize are best electrolyte drink, best electrolyte powder, best hydration powder, best electrolyte mix, and best electrolyte drink mix.

From there, I would expand into comparisons, reviews, and high-intent modifiers like sugar-free, low-sugar, no sugar, and value-focused terms.

This matters beyond standard rankings because in sampled commercial prompts, Hyro had 0 out of 8 mentions in ChatGPT, 0 out of 8 in Gemini, and 0 out of 8 in Google AI Overview.

Meanwhile, category names like LMNT, Liquid I.V., Nuun, and DripDrop are already shaping the visibility layer.

So the real objective is shortlist inclusion across search and answer surfaces.

## First Move
First, ship the decision-stage pages where buyer intent is already clear.

Second, support those pages with comparisons, review reinforcement, and other proof that can travel beyond the site.

Third, make sure the publishing basics are clean: indexing, schema, canonicals, and a structure that helps those pages get found and cited.

Modeled, estimate-only traffic potential is 70,857 in the first 6 months and 191,505 in 12 months, with aggressive upside of 327,134, but I would treat that as planning guidance rather than a guarantee.

And if we want to talk revenue impact, that still requires first-party ACV/AOV and funnel inputs.

## Close
The thesis is simple: Hyro's fastest growth move is to own the decision-stage electrolyte queries where buyers choose a product, then reinforce those pages with third-party proof.

That is how the brand becomes easier to find, compare, and mention before shoppers default to incumbents.
