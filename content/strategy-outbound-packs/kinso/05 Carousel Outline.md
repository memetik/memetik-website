# Kinso - Carousel Outline

## Traffic

### Slide 01 — Kinso does not need broader awareness first
The immediate gap is not top-of-funnel noise. It is visibility in the decision-stage moments that determine shortlist inclusion.

### Slide 02 — The market is bigger than Kinso's current footprint
Kinso currently has 599 organic visits and 22 ranking keywords against a validated category opportunity of 8,250,150 searches.

### Slide 03 — The opening is still largely unbranded
94.3% of the modeled demand mix is non-competitor and unbranded, which means buyers are still asking open category questions rather than only searching brand names.

### Slide 04 — Buyer guides are the wedge
Buyer Guides are the largest priority cluster, with 4,537,700 in demand. That is where Kinso can earn attention during evaluation instead of waiting for brand awareness to arrive first.

### Slide 05 — What to ship first
Start with decision-stage pages: buyer guides, shared inbox pages, email triage pages, and comparisons tied to real workflow choices. Then reinforce them with reviews, mentions, and backlinks.

### Slide 06 — Use the model correctly
The traffic upside is estimate-only, not a guarantee. But the strategic read is clear: Kinso needs presence where buyers decide. Comment "Kinso" for the first 3-5 pages I would prioritize.

## Revenue

### Slide 01 — Kinso's search gap is really a shortlist gap
If buyers do not see Kinso during evaluation, fewer qualified conversations ever make it to the shortlist stage.

### Slide 02 — The high-value layer is already visible in the strategy
Buyer Guides are the top priority cluster, and Alternatives & Comparisons are also Phase 1 priorities. That means the first win sits in the evaluation layer.

### Slide 03 — The current commercial signal is weak
Kinso has 599 organic visits, 22 ranking keywords, and no ranked commercial keywords were found in the approved brief.

### Slide 04 — Why this matters now
Larger incumbents like Front, Help Scout, and Missive already have materially larger search footprints, so they are better positioned to shape shortlist formation unless Kinso builds decision-stage visibility.

### Slide 05 — What to publish first
Prioritize shared inbox software pages, email triage pages, buyer guides, and comparison pages tied to real workflow choices. The goal is more evaluation presence, not vanity traffic.

### Slide 06 — Keep the public claim disciplined
Do not promise revenue from public data alone. Revenue planning requires Kinso's ACV/AOV and funnel inputs. Comment "Kinso" if you want the first pages I would ship for shortlist capture.

## AI Visibility

### Slide 01 — Kinso is missing in sampled AI answers
In sampled checks, Kinso was absent across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews for buying prompts tied to software selection.

### Slide 02 — Why that matters
Buyers do not only search anymore. They also ask answer engines which shared inbox or email triage tools they should consider.

### Slide 03 — The category is being framed by incumbents
Sampled prompt evidence showed incumbents such as Front and Help Scout appearing instead, which means the shortlist can form without Kinso in the set.

### Slide 04 — Generic publishing will not fix it
Kinso needs decision-stage pages that answer buying prompts clearly, plus reviews, community mentions, list inclusions, editorial coverage, and backlinks that reinforce the same story.

### Slide 05 — What changes retrieval
The approved strategy is a combined play: owned decision pages plus third-party proof layers that make Kinso easier to retrieve and easier to trust.
