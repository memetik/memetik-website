# Kinso - Loom Script

## Working Title
Why Kinso should win decision-stage visibility before chasing broader awareness

## Opener
If I were advising Kinso, I would not start with a broad awareness campaign.

I would start with the fact that Kinso is still too hard to find in the exact moments buyers form a shortlist.

That applies across both search and the sampled answer-engine checks.

## Breakdown
Kinso currently has 599 organic visits and 22 ranking keywords in a category with 8,250,150 validated searches.

94.3% of the modeled demand mix is non-competitor and unbranded, so the opening is still in buyer education and evaluation, not just brand capture.

Buyer Guides are the largest priority cluster, which is a strong signal that the first wedge should be decision-stage content.

No ranked commercial keywords were found in the approved brief, which reinforces that Kinso is not visible enough in the evaluation layer today.

In sampled checks, Kinso was also absent across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews, while larger incumbents already hold more search footprint and show up more often in those buying conversations.

So the issue is not just traffic. It is whether Kinso gets considered when buyers ask what tool they should shortlist.

## First Move
Start with the first 3-5 decision-stage pages: buyer guides, shared inbox software pages, email triage pages, and comparison pages tied to real workflow choices.

Make those pages specific enough to answer the questions buyers ask when they are narrowing options, not just browsing the category.

Reinforce those pages with reviews, list inclusions, community mentions, editorial coverage, and backlinks so Kinso is easier to trust across both search and answer surfaces.

Use modeled traffic only as estimate-only upside. If the conversation moves to revenue planning, say clearly that it requires Kinso's ACV/AOV and funnel inputs.

## Close
The thesis is simple: Kinso does not need broader awareness first. It needs visibility in the decision-stage conversations that create shortlist inclusion.

That is the fastest way to improve discoverability across both search and sampled answer-engine surfaces without inventing claims.
