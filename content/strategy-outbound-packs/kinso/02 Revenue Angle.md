# Kinso - Revenue Angle

## Core Angle
Kinso's visibility gap is a pipeline problem before it is a reporting problem: if buyers do not see Kinso during evaluation, fewer qualified conversations make it to the shortlist stage.

## Why It Matters Now
The first cluster to win is the decision layer, where buyers compare options and narrow choices. Kinso is not visible enough there today, and no public revenue model should be invented from that. But it is reasonable to say that missed shortlist presence limits the volume of buying conversations Kinso can earn.

## Approved Proof Points
- Buyer Guides are the top priority cluster, with 4,537,700 in demand and 499,147 modeled 12-month traffic in the base case.
- Alternatives & Comparisons are also a Phase 1 priority, which signals that evaluation-stage content matters early.
- Commercial keyword signals show no ranked commercial keywords found for Kinso today.
- Kinso currently has 599 organic visits and 22 ranking keywords, which is low relative to the size of the category opportunity.
- Competitors such as Front, Help Scout, and Missive already have much larger search footprints.
- Revenue planning requires Kinso's ACV/AOV and funnel inputs, so public messaging should stay focused on visibility, evaluation, and shortlist capture rather than guaranteed revenue.

## Claims To Avoid
- Do not claim specific revenue gains, payback periods, or pipeline dollars.
- Do not imply that traffic automatically converts to revenue.
- Do not say Kinso is losing a known amount of revenue unless first-party funnel data exists.
- Do not overstate commercial certainty; keep it at shortlist and evaluation-stage visibility.
