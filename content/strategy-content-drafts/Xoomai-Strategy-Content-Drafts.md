# XoomAI Strategy Content Drafts

## Source Angle
- Primary hook: XoomAI’s biggest missed growth opportunity is not competitor brand traffic. It is the open market. Right now, 97.6% of the validated demand is unbranded, but XoomAI is effectively invisible in both search and sampled AI answers.
- Why it matters now: Buyers researching AI workflow automation and managed AI support are already asking direct selection questions. Today, those answers are being absorbed by generic tools, large platforms, and consultancies with indexed commercial pages. If XoomAI does not build the decision-stage layer now, it stays out of shortlist consideration while the market forms around someone else.
- Proof points to use: Current organic traffic: 0. Current organic keywords: 0. Sampled AI visibility: 0/16 mention rate across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overview. Total validated demand: 14,845,730. Non-competitor / unbranded demand: 14,494,820 (97.6%). First 6-month target: 114,910 modeled visits. 12-month base case: 310,568 modeled visits. Sampled AI answers currently route users toward tools and platforms like Zapier, UiPath, Lindy, Devin, GPT-based agents, Perplexity, and Claude rather than XoomAI.
- Caveats / claims to avoid: Do not claim guaranteed revenue. Do not imply full-platform AI visibility beyond the validated sampled surfaces. Do not say XoomAI already has authority momentum. If commercial upside comes up, say revenue planning needs XoomAI’s ACV/AOV and funnel inputs.
- Suggested CTA: Ask founders whether they are building for the buyer’s research moment or waiting until the buyer already has a shortlist.

## Rough Video Script
### Hook
XoomAI does not have a traffic problem first.

It has a visibility problem.

There are nearly 14.8 million validated searches in this market.

97.6% of that is unbranded demand.

And XoomAI is currently at zero organic traffic, zero organic keywords, and zero sampled mentions across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overview.

### Body
That matters because buyers are already researching this category without brand loyalty.

They are asking things like:
best AI tools,
AI workflow automation tools,
best managed AI employee options.

And right now, those answers are going to generic software, big platforms, and better-indexed competitors.

In sampled AI answers, the market gets framed around Zapier, UiPath, Lindy, Devin, GPT-based agents, Perplexity, and Claude.

Not XoomAI.

So the opening move is not broad “AI thought leadership.”

It is decision-stage pages that help buyers choose.

Buyer guides.
Comparison pages.
Use-case pages.
Pricing and selection content.

Then each of those pages needs third-party proof around it so XoomAI is not only visible on its own website, but credible wherever buyers validate options.

If that work ships well, the modeled target is 114,910 visits in the first 6 months and 310,568 in 12 months.

Revenue planning is possible, but it needs XoomAI’s ACV/AOV and funnel inputs.

### CTA
If you’re a founder, ask yourself one question:

When buyers research your category today, are they finding you, or just finding the market without you?

If you want, comment “visibility” and I’ll break down the wedge I’d build first for XoomAI.

## LinkedIn Post

Most AI consultancies think the opportunity is “ranking for AI.”

That’s too broad to be useful.

For XoomAI, the real missed opportunity is simpler:

The market is mostly unbranded, but XoomAI is currently invisible inside it.

Validated demand in the category sits at 14,845,730 searches.

14,494,820 of that — 97.6% — is non-competitor / unbranded demand.

So this is not a market where growth depends on stealing another company’s brand traffic.

It’s a market where buyers are asking open questions and building shortlists from what they find.

Right now, XoomAI has:
0 organic traffic
0 organic keywords
0 sampled mentions across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overview

That means the issue is not lack of category demand.

It’s lack of decision-stage surface area.

And in sampled AI answers, buyers are being routed toward generic tools and platforms like Zapier, UiPath, Lindy, Devin, GPT-based agents, Perplexity, and Claude instead of XoomAI.

The fix is not more generic content.

It’s shipping the pages that match buyer selection intent first:
buyer guides, comparison pages, use-case pages, and pricing/support content — then reinforcing them with third-party proof.

Done well, the modeled upside is 114,910 visits in 6 months and 310,568 in 12 months.

But revenue planning still needs XoomAI’s ACV/AOV and funnel inputs.

If you were advising XoomAI, would you start with broad AI awareness or decision-stage buyer guides first?
