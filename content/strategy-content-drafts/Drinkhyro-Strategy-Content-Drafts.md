# Hyro Strategy Content Drafts

## Source Angle
- Primary hook: Hyro is not losing because the electrolyte market is closed. It is losing the exact decision-stage queries where buyers choose a brand, while incumbents keep getting the search clicks and the sampled AI recommendations.
- Why it matters now: Hyro already has enough baseline authority to make a focused move, but the biggest opportunity is still open-market demand. 92.6% of the validated opportunity is non-competitor and unbranded, which means Hyro can win by owning buyer-guide and comparison queries before a shopper is committed to LMNT, Liquid I.V., or anyone else.
- Proof points to use: Hyro currently has 1,004 organic visits and 362 organic keywords; the modeled first 6-month target is 70,857 visits and the modeled 12-month traffic path is 191,505; buyer-guide demand alone is 211,170, including terms like best electrolyte drink, best electrolyte powder, and best hydration powder; in sampled commercial prompts, Hyro had 0/8 mentions across ChatGPT, 0/8 across Gemini, and 0/8 across Google AI Overview; competitors like LMNT and Liquid I.V. are the names currently shaping the recommendation layer.
- Caveats / claims to avoid: Do not claim guaranteed traffic or guaranteed revenue; do not say Hyro is invisible everywhere in AI, only that it was absent in sampled commercial prompts; do not invent revenue certainty because revenue planning still needs client ACV/AOV and funnel inputs; do not frame the opportunity as only competitor-capture when most demand is unbranded.
- Suggested CTA: Ask readers to comment if they want the first five decision-stage pages Hyro should ship.

## Rough Video Script
### Hook
Hyro does not need more broad awareness content.

It needs to win the moment a buyer asks:
what’s the best electrolyte powder?
what’s the best electrolyte drink?
what should I choose?

### Body
Right now, Hyro has 1,004 organic visits.

But the traffic gap is much bigger than that.

The modeled path is 70,857 visits in 6 months and 191,505 in 12 months if the company opens with the right pages first.

And here’s the part I think founders miss:

92.6% of this demand is unbranded.

So the real opportunity is not just stealing branded traffic from LMNT or Liquid I.V.

It’s owning the open decision layer before the customer picks a favorite brand.

That means pages like best electrolyte drink, best electrolyte powder, best hydration powder, comparison pages, and use-case pages.

There’s also a recommendation problem.

In sampled commercial prompts across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overview, Hyro wasn’t mentioned.

The incumbents were.

So this is not just an SEO issue.
It’s a visibility issue at the exact moment buyers decide.

And the fix is not random blog volume.

It’s a tight commercial footprint plus third-party proof that makes Hyro easier to rank, easier to compare, and easier to recommend.

I wouldn’t turn this into revenue certainty yet.

Revenue planning still needs client ACV/AOV and funnel inputs.
