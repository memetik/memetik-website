## Rough Video Script
### Hook
A random comparison website with one-seventh of Klaviyo's backlinks is pulling three times the traffic.

Not because their product is better.

Because they built the pages Klaviyo didn't.

### Body
Here's what's happening.

There are over fifteen million monthly searches in the email-for-e-commerce category.

Pricing queries. Platform comparisons. "Which tool should I pick" questions.

Klaviyo has twenty-six thousand referring domains. Massive authority base.

But emailvendorselection dot com — a site with under four thousand referring domains — drives six hundred fifteen thousand visits a month.

Klaviyo? Two hundred twenty-four thousand.

It gets worse when you look at AI.

Klaviyo shows up in about half of Gemini answers and a third of ChatGPT answers for category queries.

But Google AI Overviews? Six percent.

That means when a buyer asks Google's AI "which email platform for my store" — Klaviyo almost never shows up.

And AI answers are hardening. The models are locking in their defaults right now.

The fix isn't complicated. Klaviyo already has the authority.

They just haven't pointed it at the queries that actually create deals — pricing pages, comparison pages, cost breakdowns.

The buying queries.

Own those, reinforce them with third-party proof, and the AI follows.

### CTA
If you're seeing comparison sites outrank your product on your own buying queries — you're not alone.

Drop a comment. I'm curious how widespread this is.

## LinkedIn Post
A comparison site with 7× fewer backlinks than Klaviyo drives nearly 3× more organic traffic.

Not because of better content quality. Because it owns the pages buyers actually need before making a decision — pricing breakdowns, head-to-head comparisons, cost explainers.

Klaviyo has 26,000+ referring domains. One of the strongest authority bases in email marketing. But that authority is sitting dormant on the queries that shape shortlists.

The AI layer makes this worse.

Klaviyo shows up in about 50% of Gemini answers and 37% of ChatGPT answers for category queries. Sounds okay — until you check Google AI Overviews.

6%.

When a founder asks Google's AI which email platform to pick for their store, Klaviyo is almost never in the answer.

Meanwhile, 99.6% of the 15M+ monthly searches in this category are unbranded. Buyers looking for solutions, not specific tools. That's capturable demand — if you build the decision-stage content to intercept it.

The pattern isn't unique to Klaviyo. I see it across SaaS: companies with strong products and strong backlink profiles losing the buying narrative to third-party sites that built focused comparison and pricing pages.

The authority is already there. It just needs to be aimed at the right queries.

Modeled estimate: a focused strategy here could move Klaviyo from 224K to roughly 398K monthly organic visits in twelve months. Revenue impact depends on their actual deal economics — that math needs first-party conversion and contract value inputs to be real.

Anyone else watching comparison sites eat your category from underneath you? Curious what you're seeing.
