## Rough Video Script
### Hook
Kinso does not have a demand problem.

It has a visibility problem in the exact moments buyers choose a tool.

Right now, most of the shared inbox market is still open.

But Kinso is barely showing up.

### Body
Here’s the part I’d pay attention to.

Kinso is at 599 organic visits and 22 ranking keywords.

The validated category opportunity is over 8.25 million searches.

And 94.3% of that demand is unbranded.

So this is not a market where buyers have already made up their minds.

It’s a market where they’re still asking:
what’s the best shared inbox,
how should email triage work,
what tool fits our workflow.

That’s the opportunity.

The problem is that Kinso is not showing up where those decisions get shaped.

In sampled checks, Kinso had zero visibility across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews.

Meanwhile, incumbents like Front and Help Scout are framing the category.

So the first move is not broad awareness.

It’s to own the decision-stage pages:
shared inbox software,
email triage software,
comparison pages,
and buyer guides tied to real workflows.

Then attach proof around them:
reviews,
third-party mentions,
community discussion,
and backlinks.

The traffic upside is estimate-only, but the model is meaningful:
213,103 visits in the first 6 months,
575,954 in 12 months.

Revenue planning is a separate exercise and needs Kinso's ACV/AOV and funnel inputs.
