## 10 Ways to Audit Your AI Visibility

### 1. Establish Your Query Baseline

**Why it matters:** You can't measure improvement without knowing where you start. The foundation of any AI visibility audit is identifying 50-100 queries where your brand SHOULD appear based on your product category, use cases, and buyer questions.

**How to do it:** Start with three query categories. Product category queries like "best CRM for sales teams" or "marketing automation platforms for SaaS." Comparison queries like "Salesforce vs HubSpot" or "ActiveCampaign alternatives." Solution queries like "how to improve email deliverability" or "ways to reduce sales cycle length."

Use MEMETIK's query builder to auto-generate query sets based on your industry and competitors. Our system analyzes your site, identifies competitive positioning, and creates 50-100 queries organized by funnel stage and buyer intent. You can manually add queries based on sales conversations, demo questions, or support tickets—anywhere buyers reveal how they search for solutions.

**Deliverable:** A spreadsheet of target queries organized by category, intent, and priority. This becomes your baseline for all future audits. Track monthly to measure citation rate improvement over time.

### 2. Test Across Multiple AI Platforms

**Why it matters:** Each LLM has different training data, update frequencies, and citation patterns. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Google AI Overviews, and Bing Chat pull from different sources and apply different algorithms for determining what to cite. A brand might appear frequently in Perplexity but never in ChatGPT.

**How to do it:** Run identical queries across all five major platforms. For each query, document which platforms cite you, which don't, and what context surrounds your mention. Note whether you're recommended, simply mentioned, compared favorably, or positioned negatively.

Based on MEMETIK client data, brands appear 3.2x more frequently in Perplexity versus ChatGPT due to real-time web access. Google AI Overviews favor sites with strong existing search authority. Claude tends to cite longer-form educational content over product pages.

MEMETIK's automated cross-platform testing saves 20+ hours of manual querying. Our dashboard runs your query set across all platforms simultaneously, capturing screenshots and extracting citation data so you can compare platform-by-platform performance in one view.

**Deliverable:** Platform-by-platform citation matrix showing where you appear, where you don't, and which platforms represent your biggest opportunity.

### 3. Calculate Your Citation Rate

**Why it matters:** Citation rate is your north star metric for AI visibility. It's the percentage of relevant queries where AI assistants mention your brand. Industry benchmark for B2B brands is 8-12%, with top performers achieving 20-35%.

**How to do it:** The formula is simple: (number of queries with citations ÷ total queries tested) × 100 = citation rate. If you test 50 queries and your brand appears in 6 responses, your citation rate is 12%.

Track this monthly to measure improvement. A citation rate below 5% indicates a foundational visibility problem requiring content infrastructure investment. Between 10-15% means you have baseline presence but need optimization. Above 20% puts you in top performer territory.

MEMETIK's dashboard automatically calculates citation rate and shows trending over time with competitor comparison. You can filter by platform, query category, or funnel stage to identify where you're strongest and where you need work.

**Deliverable:** Monthly citation rate metric with historical trending and competitive benchmarks. This becomes your primary KPI for AEO performance.

### 4. Audit Citation Context & Accuracy

**Why it matters:** Being mentioned incorrectly is worse than not being mentioned at all. If ChatGPT tells prospects your product costs $299/month when it's actually $499, you've created an expectation problem before the first sales conversation. If Perplexity describes features you discontinued six months ago, prospects arrive confused or disappointed.

**How to do it:** Read every citation where your brand appears. Verify that pricing, features, positioning, and facts are current and accurate. Common errors we see: outdated pricing from old blog posts, discontinued features from historical documentation, wrong company descriptions from Wikipedia, or confusion with similarly-named competitors.

Document each inaccuracy with the platform, query, incorrect information, and correct information. This creates a prioritized list of content updates needed to fix AI understanding of your brand.

MEMETIK's accuracy scoring feature flags potential misinformation automatically by comparing AI responses against your current product data and website content. When we detect discrepancies, you receive alerts to investigate and correct.

**Deliverable:** Accuracy audit report listing every citation with accuracy score and correction priorities.

### 5. Map Your Competitive Share of Voice

**Why it matters:** AI visibility isn't absolute—it's competitive. If prospects ask "best project management tools for agencies" and ChatGPT recommends four competitors but never mentions you, you've lost that consideration opportunity. Share of voice shows how often you're cited versus competitors for the same queries.

**How to do it:** For each query in your baseline, record which competitors appear and in what context. Are they recommended as top options? Mentioned alongside you? Compared favorably? Track frequency by competitor to calculate share of voice.

If competitors appear 4x more often across your query set, you're losing consideration opportunities at scale. This reveals which competitors have stronger AEO infrastructure and where you need to compete.

**Deliverable:** Competitive share of voice report showing citation frequency by competitor across your query baseline.

### 6. Identify Content Gaps

**Why it matters:** Queries where competitors get cited but you don't reveal specific content gaps. Maybe you lack comparison pages, category authority content, or comprehensive documentation. These gaps are why AI assistants overlook you.

**How to do it:** Filter your audit results for "zero citation" queries—places where AI never mentions you. For each query, analyze what content competitors have that you don't. Visit the URLs that AI platforms cite for those queries and identify patterns.

Common gaps we see: missing comparison pages (Salesforce vs [Your Product]), no category authority content ranking you in "best [category]" queries, sparse or outdated documentation, no case studies or customer stories, or missing pricing transparency.

MEMETIK's programmatic SEO approach creates 900+ pages at scale to fill these gaps within 90 days. Our system identifies missing content categories, generates AEO-optimized pages with proper structure and schema, and monitors citation improvement as new pages get indexed.

**Deliverable:** Content gap analysis prioritizing which pages to create first based on query importance and competitive difficulty.

### 7. Check Source Attribution

**Why it matters:** When AI cites you, which URLs does it reference? This reveals what content types LLMs trust and which pages earn the most citations. You can double down on high-performing formats and update underperforming content.

**How to do it:** Document which specific pages earn citations in your audit. Look for patterns across content types: blog posts, product pages, documentation, case studies, comparison pages, or resource centers.

Based on our client data, case studies earn 2.7x more citations than generic blog posts for B2B queries. Comprehensive guides (2,000+ words) outperform short-form content. Pages with embedded data, original research, or customer quotes get cited more frequently than promotional content.

MEMETIK's URL-level tracking shows which content assets perform best in AEO. You can see which pages drive the most citations, which haven't earned any despite relevance, and which need updates to maintain visibility.

**Deliverable:** Source attribution report ranking your top-performing pages by citation frequency and identifying update priorities.

### 8. Evaluate Structured Data Coverage

**Why it matters:** Schema markup increases AI citation probability by 214%. Structured data helps LLMs understand what your content says, who published it, when it was updated, and what entities it references. Pages without schema are harder for AI to parse and less likely to be cited.

**How to do it:** Audit which pages have Article, FAQPage, HowTo, Product, or Organization schema implemented. Use Google Rich Results Test to validate JSON-LD implementation. Check that schema includes critical fields like datePublished, dateModified, author credentials, and factual claims.

Priority schema types for B2B: Article schema on blog posts and guides, FAQPage schema on support content, HowTo schema on tutorials, Product schema on solution pages, and Organization schema on about/company pages.

MEMETIK's AEO-first approach builds schema into every page template. Our programmatic SEO system automatically generates properly structured JSON-LD for each content type, ensuring AI platforms can easily parse and cite your content.

**Deliverable:** Schema coverage audit showing which pages have proper markup and which need implementation.

### 9. Assess Content Freshness

**Why it matters:** 68% of AI citations reference content from the last 12 months. Stale content signals to LLMs that information may be outdated, reducing citation probability. Fresh content with recent publication or update dates earns preferential treatment.

**How to do it:** Audit when key pages were last updated. Flag content older than 6 months in critical categories (product pages, pricing, comparisons, category pages). Check if your highest-value pages have visible "Last updated" dates.

Quick win: Add "Last updated: [date]" to pages and commit to refreshing statistics, examples, and data points quarterly. Update copyright years, refresh screenshots, and add recent customer stories or case studies.

MEMETIK implements 90-day content refresh cycles to maintain citation velocity. Our system flags pages that haven't been updated recently, suggests refresh opportunities based on citation performance, and tracks whether updates improve AI visibility.

**Deliverable:** Content freshness audit with update priorities based on page importance and current staleness.

**Why it matters:** AI visibility audit isn't one-time—it requires monthly tracking like traditional SEO. LLMs update training data, competitors publish new content, and your citation rate fluctuates. Without ongoing monitoring, you can't catch drops in visibility before they impact pipeline.

**How to do it:** Schedule monthly query runs tracking your core 50-100 queries. Monitor citation rate trending month-over-month. Set alerts for significant drops (>10% citation rate decrease) or new competitor mentions. Review accuracy monthly to catch emerging misinformation.
