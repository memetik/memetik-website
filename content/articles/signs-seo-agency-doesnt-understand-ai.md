## The 15 Warning Signs

### Sign #1: They Can't Track LLM Citations

Your agency should show you exactly where and how often your brand appears in responses from ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews. If their reporting dashboard doesn't include LLM citation metrics, they're flying blind.

You can't optimize what you don't measure. Traditional agencies focus on keyword rankings because that's what their tools measure—not because it reflects where buyers actually search. We track citations across 40+ query variations on four LLM platforms because AI visibility is now the primary indicator of search authority.

### Sign #2: Still Obsessing Over Keyword Density

Monthly reports that highlight "keyword density" or "keyword frequency" are red flags. If your agency celebrates hitting a 2.5% density target for "best CRM software," they fundamentally misunderstand how AI search works.

LLMs understand semantic meaning and entity relationships, not keyword stuffing. They analyze whether your content actually answers questions naturally. An article repeating "best CRM software" 47 times ranks lower than one naturally addressing "What CRM features do growing sales teams need?" The modern approach uses entity-based optimization and topic clusters that mirror human conversation.

### Sign #3: Ignoring Structured Data Beyond Basics

Check your website's schema implementation. If your agency has only added Organization and Article schema, they're missing 90% of AI optimization opportunities.

Average agencies implement 2-3 schema types. AI-visible sites use 15+, including FAQ, HowTo, Product, SoftwareApplication, Review, VideoObject, and more. LLMs heavily weight structured data when extracting answers because it provides explicit semantic signals about content meaning and relationships. We implement comprehensive schema across every content type because it directly impacts citation probability.

### Sign #4: No Strategy for Zero-Click Optimization

If all your agency's KPIs focus on click-through rate and website traffic, they're ignoring 57% of search opportunities. Zero-click searches—where users get answers without clicking—now represent the majority of Google queries and virtually all AI assistant interactions.

What's missing? Featured snippet optimization. People Also Ask targeting. AI Overview inclusion strategy. Entity optimization for direct answer extraction. These require completely different content architecture than traditional SEO, and most agencies don't have frameworks for zero-click visibility.

### Sign #5: They're Still Talking About "10 Blue Links"

Listen carefully during strategy presentations. If your agency references SERP positions 1-10 as primary goals, they're optimizing for a search experience that's rapidly disappearing.

Google AI Overviews appear above position 1, consuming 40% of screen real estate on mobile. LLMs don't have "positions"—they either cite you or they don't. The modern mindset focuses on multi-surface visibility: traditional SERP presence, AI Overview inclusion, and LLM citations across platforms. Agencies stuck in the "10 blue links" mental model are optimizing for 2018.

### Sign #6: Backlink Strategy Hasn't Evolved

Traditional agencies still chase domain authority metrics and generic backlinks from high-DR sites. They'll celebrate securing a link from a DR-70 website, regardless of citation relevance.

LLMs prioritize citation-worthy, frequently-updated, structured sources—not domain authority scores. A link from a site that ChatGPT regularly cites is worth 10x more than a generic high-DR backlink. The evolution: citation-optimized content architectures outperform traditional link-building for AI visibility. Ask yourself: "How likely is this content to be cited by an LLM?" That's the metric that matters.

### Sign #7: Content Word Count Obsession

Agencies that mandate 2,500+ word articles because "longer equals better rankings" misunderstand AI search fundamentally. They're optimizing for outdated Google algorithm assumptions, not LLM citation behavior.

LLMs prefer concise, direct answers with clear structure over lengthy keyword-stuffed content. An 800-word article with perfect schema markup and entity optimization outperforms a 3,000-word rambling piece in AI results. The balance: comprehensive coverage with scannable structure. Answer the question completely but efficiently.

### Sign #8: No Programmatic Content Strategy

Traditional agencies produce 4-8 manually-written articles per month. That pace worked for 2015 SEO. It fails completely for AI search.

The scale problem: AI visibility requires 500+ optimized pages covering entity variations across your topic universe. Sites with 900+ pages show 12x higher LLM citation rates because they provide comprehensive entity coverage that LLMs recognize as authoritative sources. We deploy programmatic SEO infrastructure that creates hundreds of entity-optimized pages automatically—something manual content agencies physically cannot match.

### Sign #9: Can't Explain E-E-A-T for AI

Agencies reference Google's E-E-A-T guidelines (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) but can't articulate how LLMs actually evaluate source credibility.

What's missing: structured author entities with verified credentials, expertise schema markup, citation-worthy fact presentation with primary source links, and entity-relationship establishment. Modern E-E-A-T for AI requires explicit semantic signals—author bio schema, organizational credentials, expertise indicators that LLMs can parse and evaluate programmatically.

### Sign #10: Not Optimizing for Conversational Queries

Keyword research spreadsheets still focus on 2-3 word phrases like "CRM software" or "project management tool." This ignores how people actually use AI search.

Voice and AI searches are full questions: "What's the best CRM for a 50-person sales team under $10,000 per year?" The gap: no long-tail conversational query optimization. No natural language pattern analysis. No entity relationship mapping. AI-ready content answers questions the way buyers ask them, using natural language processing to identify question patterns and entity relationships.

### Sign #11: No Testing in AI Platforms

Simple test: Ask your agency to show you how your brand performs for key queries in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude. If they've never tested it, they can't optimize for it.

We run regular AI search audits, testing 40+ query variations across platforms monthly. We track competitive AI visibility, citation context, and answer quality. You can't improve visibility in channels you don't monitor. This isn't optional—it's foundational to AEO methodology.

### Sign #12: Generic "AI Content" Claims

Red flag phrase: "We use ChatGPT to write your blog posts—that's our AI strategy."

This reveals fundamental confusion. AI-generated content is not the same as AI search optimization. Using ChatGPT as a writing tool has nothing to do with getting your brand cited BY ChatGPT. AEO focuses on becoming a trusted source that LLMs reference, not on using LLMs to create content. If your agency can't explain this distinction, they don't understand Answer Engine Optimization.

### Sign #13: Monthly Reports Show Vanity Metrics Only

Open your last three monthly reports. Do they show traffic, keyword rankings, and impressions—but nothing about AI visibility?

Missing metrics: featured snippet captures, AI Overview appearances, LLM citation tracking, zero-click impression share, entity coverage scores. Modern dashboards must reflect multi-surface visibility. If your agency can't report where you appear in AI Overviews or how often ChatGPT cites your brand, you're measuring the wrong things.

### Sign #14: No Speed or Core Web Vitals Urgency

Your site scores poorly on PageSpeed Insights, but your agency says "it's fine, we're ranking." This dismissiveness reveals they don't understand current ranking factors or LLM source selection.

Page speed affects crawl priority and influences whether LLMs select your content as source material. Google's Core Web Vitals are confirmed ranking factors, especially for AI Overview inclusion. All pages should achieve under 2.5 seconds Largest Contentful Paint. Slow sites get excluded from AI search experiences regardless of content quality.

### Sign #15: Can't Provide an AI Visibility Guarantee

The ultimate test: Ask your agency to guarantee AI visibility results within a specific timeframe. If they deflect with "SEO takes time" or "we can't guarantee rankings," they don't have a proven methodology.

We offer a 90-day AI visibility guarantee—measurable LLM citations or money back—because our programmatic AEO framework delivers predictable results.
