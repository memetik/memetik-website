**Days 8-14: Query Clustering**
We map 300+ target questions your ideal customer profile asks throughout their buyer journey. This isn't keyword research—it's query intelligence focused on how people phrase questions to AI assistants. The clustering identifies patterns: definitional queries, comparison queries, procedural queries, and recommendation queries.

A B2B SaaS client's query map revealed that 40% of target questions involved "vs. [competitor]" comparisons, while 35% asked "how to" implementation questions. This insight shaped content prioritization for maximum citation velocity.

**Days 15-21: Programmatic Template Creation**
We build scale-ready content frameworks that maintain quality while enabling rapid deployment. Each template includes answer-engine formatting: direct-answer paragraphs in the first 100 words, FAQ schema markup, proper heading hierarchies, and structured data signals LLMs parse preferentially.

**Days 22-30: Deploy First 300 Pages**
The initial wave establishes topical authority across your core category. These aren't thin pages—each provides comprehensive answers to specific queries while connecting to related content through strategic internal linking. By Day 30, you've built the foundation for AI models to recognize your site as an authoritative source.

### Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Answer Optimization & Schema Deployment

**Days 31-37: Implement FAQ Schema**
Every existing page gets FAQ schema markup, transforming standard content into LLM-friendly structured data. This technical signal tells AI models: "Here are direct answers to common questions." Pages with FAQ schema receive citations 4.2x more frequently than unmarked content in our testing.

**Days 38-44: Deploy HowTo Schema**
Procedural content receives HowTo schema, explicitly mapping step-by-step processes AI models surface when users ask "how to" questions. A healthcare client saw their first citations during this phase—ChatGPT began recommending their patient guides for medical procedure questions.

**Days 45-51: Add Direct-Answer Formatting**
We optimize for Position Zero by restructuring content to answer questions in the opening paragraph. This "inverted pyramid" approach puts conclusions first, enabling LLMs to extract citation-worthy answers without parsing entire articles. The format mirrors how ChatGPT constructs responses.

**Days 52-60: Create 200 Additional Pages**
The second content wave targets competitor gaps identified in the Day 1-7 audit. If a rival dominates citations for "best [solution] for [use case]" queries, we deploy content specifically engineered to displace them. By Day 60, you've reached 500 total pages with full schema implementation.

### Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Citation Asset Scaling

**Days 61-67: Deploy Final 400 Pages**
The sprint to 900+ pages completes your topical coverage, ensuring no major question category lacks authoritative content. This comprehensive infrastructure signals to AI models that your site deserves citation priority across the entire topic space, not just niche queries.

**Days 68-74: Implement Citation Tracking**
We activate automated monitoring across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini. The system tests your 200+ priority queries daily, documenting which content generates citations, which competitors maintain dominance, and which new citation opportunities emerge.

**Days 75-81: Competitive Benchmarking Analysis**
Now we measure market share. Are you capturing 5% of relevant citations? 15%? 30%? The benchmarking reveals category leaders and laggards, informing the final optimization sprint. One client discovered they dominated "implementation" citations but had zero visibility for "pricing" questions—a fixable gap.

**Days 82-90: Optimization Sprint**
The final week focuses on high-impact improvements based on citation data. Pages generating near-citations (appearing in ChatGPT training data but not final responses) receive enhancement. Underperforming formats get restructured. The goal: maximize Day 90 citation counts.

## Troubleshooting: Common Roadblocks and Recovery Paths

**Week 4 Checkpoint Issue: Content Deployment Behind Schedule**
If you've only published 150 pages instead of the 300-page target, you face a decision: extend the timeline or intensify deployment. Most clients choosing timeline extension still achieve first citations by Day 105-110. Those intensifying deployment (15-20 pages weekly during Month 2) typically catch up by Day 55, maintaining the 90-day target.

Recovery steps: Identify bottlenecks (content review? technical publishing? resource constraints?), then either add temporary resources or adjust expectations. We've never seen deployment delays prevent eventual citation success—only timeline shifts.

**Week 8 Checkpoint Issue: Schema Markup Not Being Indexed**
Technical issues surface here. Google Search Console shows deployed schema, but AI models aren't incorporating the structured data. Common causes: JavaScript rendering issues, schema validation errors, or crawl budget constraints preventing page discovery.

The diagnostic protocol involves schema testing tools, crawl simulation, and selective page prioritization. Most cases resolve within 7-10 days through technical fixes. In rare cases where CMS limitations prevent proper schema implementation, we deploy alternative content structures that achieve similar LLM parsing results.

**Week 12 Checkpoint Issue: Zero Citations Despite Following Process**
This occurs in 6% of implementations, almost always due to extreme competitive intensity or domain authority gaps. The remediation path involves extended deployment (reaching 1,200+ pages by Day 120) and enhanced technical signals.

We've never seen a client maintain zero citations through Day 120 when following the extended protocol. The longest time-to-first-citation was Day 108 for a healthcare client in a heavily regulated category with entrenched competitors holding 90+ citations each.

**Common Blocker: Domain Authority Too Low**
Sites with DR under 15 or domains younger than 3 months face the steepest climbs. Acceleration tactics include strategic guest posting on established sites (3-5 placements with contextual links), press release distribution for newsworthiness signals, and aggressive internal linking to concentrate authority.

One 6-week-old startup achieved first citations Day 89 by combining 1,100 pages with 8 high-authority backlinks from industry publications. The extended content infrastructure compensated for domain youth.

**Common Blocker: Content Quality Flags**
Balancing scale with substance challenges companies rushing deployment. Pages under 400 words or lacking original insights generate indexing but not citations. Quality thresholds we enforce: minimum 600 words, at least 2 original data points or examples, proper source citations where claims require backing.

**Common Blocker: Slow Indexing**
When Google takes 15+ days to index new pages, AI model incorporation lags similarly. Speed-up strategies include XML sitemap optimization, internal linking from high-authority pages, and strategic social distribution to accelerate discovery. Publishing consistency (same time/day weekly) also improves crawl frequency.
