Selecting an answer engine optimization agency differs fundamentally from hiring traditional SEO firms. Language model behavior, citation patterns, and AI visibility metrics require different evaluation criteria than search engine rankings and backlink profiles.

Meaningful visibility improvements materialize in months 4-5 as language models establish consistent citation patterns. Plan for minimum six-month investment regardless of agency selection.

Companies with annual marketing budgets under $100,000 should allocate 25-35% to answer engine optimization given AI's accelerating share of search volume. Enterprise organizations often maintain separate AEO budgets entirely.

### Essential Discovery Call Questions

**"Which specific language models do you track for citations, and can you show me your tracking dashboard?"**

This question separates genuine AEO agencies from SEO firms adding "AI optimization" to service descriptions. Legitimate agencies demonstrate purpose-built citation tracking tools showing visibility across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, and other major LLMs. Screenshots of generic analytics dashboards without AI-specific metrics indicate lack of specialized capability.

**"What's your content production methodology—manual creation, AI-assisted, or programmatic?"**

Production methodology determines scalability and pricing efficiency. Manual content creation, even with AI assistance, cannot achieve volume required for comprehensive answer engine coverage without exponential cost increases. Programmatic methodologies enable scale at controlled costs. Understanding production approach clarifies realistic deliverable expectations.

**"Can you provide examples of AI citations you've generated for existing clients?"**

Agencies should readily share anonymized examples showing their content cited in ChatGPT responses, Perplexity answers, or Claude outputs. Hesitation or inability to provide examples suggests limited track record. Request examples across multiple language models to verify breadth of expertise.

**"What performance guarantees or accountability measures do you offer?"**

Only 27% of affordable AEO agencies offer formal performance guarantees. Agencies confident in their methodologies provide 90-day citation guarantees, visibility SLAs, or refund structures. Lack of guarantees doesn't necessarily indicate poor quality, but presence of guarantees signals confidence and accountability.

**"How do you optimize content differently for ChatGPT versus Claude versus Perplexity?"**

Language models exhibit different citation behaviors, information preferences, and source evaluation patterns. ChatGPT favors comprehensive overview content. Claude prioritizes nuanced analysis and primary sources. Perplexity emphasizes recency and multi-source corroboration. Agencies should articulate model-specific optimization strategies, not generic "AI content optimization."

**"What's your typical timeline from engagement start to first measurable citations?"**

Realistic agencies cite 60-90 day timelines for initial citations, with significant visibility improvements by months 4-5. Promises of citations within 30 days likely indicate either unrealistic expectations or gaming of metrics (citations in obscure queries that don't drive business value). Understanding realistic timelines prevents disappointment and premature agency changes.

**"How do you measure AEO success beyond citation counts?"**

Citation volume provides incomplete success measurement. Effective agencies track citation quality (relevance to business objectives), query coverage (breadth of topics generating citations), and answer engine referral traffic. Agencies focused solely on citation counts may optimize for vanity metrics rather than business outcomes.

**No dedicated AI citation tracking tool** signals fundamental capability gaps. Manual citation checking through sporadic query testing cannot provide comprehensive visibility measurement or identify optimization opportunities systematically. Purpose-built tracking infrastructure is non-negotiable for legitimate AEO services.

ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini exhibit distinct citation patterns, information preferences, and source evaluation criteria. Agencies treating all LLMs identically lack specialized expertise.

**Inability to explain programmatic content methodology** limits scalability potential. Traditional content creation—even AI-assisted—cannot achieve volume required for comprehensive answer engine coverage at affordable price points. Agencies without programmatic capabilities either deliver insufficient content volume or charge premium pricing for manual production.

**Lack of schema markup expertise** undermines content discoverability by AI models. Structured data helps language models understand content context, entity relationships, and information hierarchy. Agencies treating schema as afterthought miss critical technical foundation for AEO effectiveness.
