## Complete Buying Guide for AI-First Content Agencies

### Step 1: Establish Your AI Visibility Baseline

Before agency conversations, document your current AI visibility to establish improvement benchmarks and evaluate agency claims objectively.

**Test your brand across major AI assistants** with queries your buyers actually use:

- "best [your category] for [specific use case]"
- "compare [your brand] vs [competitor]"
- "[specific feature] in [your category]"
- "what's the best [category] for [industry]"

Run 15-20 queries across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini. Document whether your brand appears, how it's positioned, and what context surrounds mentions. Most companies discover 0-15% citation rates—meaning AI assistants ignore their brand in 85-100% of relevant queries.

**This baseline enables objective agency evaluation.** When agencies claim they'll improve AI visibility, you can measure actual progress versus starting point. Without baseline documentation, you're evaluating vibes rather than results.

### Step 2: Validate Genuine AI-First Capabilities

Agency websites uniformly claim "AI-powered" capabilities. Separate genuine infrastructure from marketing claims by asking specific validation questions:

**"Show me your AI citation tracking dashboard for an existing client."**

Genuine AI-first agencies maintain real-time dashboards monitoring LLM mentions across multiple platforms. They should demonstrate actual citation tracking, not describe theoretical capabilities. If agencies cannot show dashboards, they lack systematic monitoring infrastructure.

**"What's your average monthly content production volume?"**

Traditional agencies produce 4-16 pieces monthly through editorial processes. AI-first agencies produce 50-500+ pages through programmatic infrastructure. Answers under 30 pieces monthly indicate traditional approaches inadequate for comprehensive AEO.

**"Do you have LLM testing infrastructure, or manually check AI responses?"**

Systematic testing requires automated prompt libraries, regular testing cycles across multiple LLMs, and optimization workflows triggered by results. Manual spot-checking indicates absence of genuine testing infrastructure.

**"What percentage of content strategy is programmatic versus editorial?"**

AI-first agencies should describe 60-80% programmatic content generation addressing long-tail queries, with 20-40% editorial thought leadership. Traditional agencies describe primarily editorial approaches with "some programmatic elements."

**"Can you guarantee AEO visibility within 90 days?"**

Only agencies with proven infrastructure offer contractual guarantees. "We cannot guarantee results" disclaimers indicate traditional approaches unable to reliably deliver within specific timeframes. MEMETIK's 90-day guarantee reflects confidence in our programmatic systems—not marketing hyperbole.

### Step 3: Evaluate Programmatic Infrastructure

Programmatic content infrastructure separates AI-first agencies from traditional firms attempting scale through hiring more writers. Ask detailed questions about technology capabilities:

**"What CMS and automation systems enable your content production?"**

Look for custom platforms, headless CMS implementations, or sophisticated WordPress architectures enabling variant generation. Standard WordPress installations with manual publishing indicate traditional editorial approaches.

**"Show me examples of programmatic content campaigns—city pages, comparison matrices, use-case libraries."**

Request actual examples demonstrating intelligent variation generation, not templated content with find-and-replace city names. Quality programmatic content maintains optimization while varying specifics based on parameters.
