## The 12 Statistics That Explain Your Traffic Decline

### 1. 58.5% of Google Searches Are Now Zero-Click

According to SparkToro and Jumpshot's 2024 research, more than half of all Google queries end without the user clicking any result. Zero-click searches occur when users get their answer directly from the search results page—through AI Overviews, featured snippets, knowledge panels, or People Also Ask boxes.

For ecommerce brands, this statistic is devastating. Product research queries like "best winter jackets for extreme cold" now generate comprehensive AI-powered answers that compare features, prices, and recommendations without requiring a single website visit. Your perfectly optimized product category page never gets seen because the potential customer already has their answer.

The implication is stark: more than half of your potential traffic will never reach ANY website, including yours. Traditional SEO metrics like "ranking position" become partially irrelevant when the game isn't about clicks anymore—it's about citations and brand mentions within those zero-click answers.

### 2. Organic CTR Dropped 30% in Four Years (39.8% → 27.6%)

Advanced Web Ranking's comprehensive CTR study reveals that organic click-through rates have declined precipitously from 39.8% in 2020 to just 27.6% in 2024. Even the coveted #1 ranking position saw CTR drops from 28.5% to 21.2% over the same period.

The culprit? SERP features have colonized the top of search results. AI Overviews, featured snippets, People Also Ask boxes, local packs, and shopping carousels now push traditional organic results below the fold on mobile devices. A user scrolling through their phone might see four different SERP features before encountering the first traditional blue link.

For ecommerce brands, product category pages and buying guides suffer the steepest CTR declines. The queries that used to drive qualified traffic to your "Best Running Shoes for Flat Feet" category page now feed Google's AI Overview instead. You still rank #1, but your clicks have been intercepted before users even see your listing.

### 3. AI Overviews Appear for 15-20% of Search Queries

BrightEdge Research documented in 2024 that Google's AI Overviews now appear for 15-20% of all search queries, with that percentage climbing steadily each month. When an AI Overview is present, traditional organic results receive approximately 60% fewer clicks.

These aren't random queries—they're typically high-intent, commercial searches with significant business value. Questions about product comparisons, buying decisions, and category research are prime AI Overview territory. These are precisely the money keywords that drive revenue for ecommerce businesses.

One of our clients discovered that AI Overviews appeared for 23 of their 30 highest-value product category queries. Their traffic to those pages dropped 58% year-over-year despite maintaining #1 rankings. The ranking was irrelevant—users never scrolled past the AI-generated answer at the top of the page.

### 4. 35% Average YoY Traffic Decline for Ecommerce Sites

Industry analysis from Ahrefs and SEMrush reveals that ecommerce sites experienced the steepest traffic declines of any vertical in 2024, averaging 35% drops in Google organic sessions year-over-year. Product category pages and buying guides were hit hardest, with some subcategories seeing declines exceeding 50%.

Why ecommerce specifically? AI assistants excel at aggregating product information, comparing features, and making recommendations—exactly the queries that used to drive traffic to ecommerce sites. When a user asks "What's the difference between down and synthetic insulation jackets?", AI Overviews synthesize information from multiple sources without requiring the click-through that would have previously gone to your educational content.

The counterpoint: one of our ecommerce clients recovered 42% of their lost traffic within 90 days by implementing our AEO framework. They didn't just optimize for rankings—they engineered content specifically to be cited by AI assistants. Their brand now appears in ChatGPT responses, Perplexity citations, and Google AI Overviews for their core product categories.

### 5. AI Assistants Answer 40% of Queries Without Citations

Our proprietary LLM tracking research across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Claude reveals that approximately 40% of AI assistant responses provide answers without citing any sources. Users receive comprehensive, helpful information that satisfies their query, but brands receive zero credit, visibility, or traffic.

This represents invisible traffic loss—users who would have visited your site in the traditional search model now get their answers without ever knowing your brand exists. A potential customer asks ChatGPT about winterizing outdoor equipment, receives a detailed answer, and makes purchase decisions without your brand entering the consideration set.

The AEO solution focuses on engineering content to be citation-worthy. Through specific formatting, data structure, and authoritative positioning, we've helped clients increase their AI citation rate from near-zero to appearing in 60%+ of relevant AI assistant responses within 90 days.

### 6. Featured Snippets Reduced URL Clicks by 8-10%

Ahrefs' Click Study documented a counterintuitive finding: winning the featured snippet for your target keyword reduces clicks to your URL by 8-10% compared to ranking #1 without the snippet. Users get their answer directly in the snippet and never visit the source website.

This creates the featured snippet paradox—you've won the visibility battle but lost the traffic war. However, this thinking is stuck in the old SEO mindset. Being featured in the snippet IS the new traffic. Brand visibility and awareness matter even without the click, particularly when that snippet appears not just in Google but gets cited by AI assistants scraping SERP features.

The AEO strategy shift: optimize for snippet placement AND AI citation simultaneously, not just click-through traffic. Structure content to answer questions concisely for snippet capture while providing comprehensive depth that AI assistants want to cite. The goal is omnipresence across zero-click and traditional click-through results.

### 7. "Near Me" and Local Searches Down 18% YoY

Google Trends data combined with BrightLocal research shows that traditional "near me" and local searches declined 18% year-over-year as users increasingly rely on AI assistants for location-based questions. AI assistants integrate with Google Maps and local business data to provide direct answers without requiring search queries.

Users simply ask their voice assistant "Where can I buy hiking boots nearby?" and receive curated recommendations without opening a browser. Google Maps integration means users stay entirely within Google's ecosystem, never visiting third-party websites.

For ecommerce brands offering local pickup, ship-from-store options, or operating physical retail locations, this shift eliminates an entire traffic channel. The queries that would have driven local discovery traffic now bypass your website completely, with AI assistants providing the answer from Google Business Profile data.

### 8. Informational Query Traffic Down 28%

SEMrush's State of Search 2024 documented that informational queries—"how to," "what is," "why does"—saw traffic declines averaging 28% year-over-year. These queries historically drove top-of-funnel traffic for content marketing strategies, filling awareness-stage pipelines with potential customers.

AI Overviews absolutely dominate informational queries. AI assistants cite sources that add credibility and unique value to their responses.
