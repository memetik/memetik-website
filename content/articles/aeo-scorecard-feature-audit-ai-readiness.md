## How the AEO Scorecard Analysis Works

The audit process runs automatically once you submit your domain, requiring minimal involvement from your team. Here's exactly what happens during those 72 hours:

**Step 1: Domain Connection and Crawl Initiation**

After simple domain verification (either through DNS or Google Search Console connection), our crawler begins analyzing your website. We can process up to 10,000 pages per domain, though most audits focus on the top 100-500 pages by authority—the content most likely to drive AI citations. The system identifies your site structure, content hierarchy, and existing optimization baseline.

**Step 2: Content Analysis Against 23 Quality Factors**

Our algorithms evaluate how well your content matches AI-preferred answer formats. We measure answer density, examining how many direct questions you address per article. We assess entity connectivity, mapping how thoroughly you link related concepts. Data richness scores reflect your use of specific statistics, examples, and concrete details rather than generic claims.

Source quality matters—we check whether you cite authoritative references, as LLMs favor content that demonstrates research depth. Format analysis identifies FAQ sections, structured Q&A patterns, and clear answer hierarchies that AI systems can easily parse.

One client discovered their content scored only 31/100 on data richness despite publishing extensively. The issue? Their articles made broad claims without specific statistics. After adding concrete data points, their content scores jumped to 68/100 within 60 days.

**Step 3: Brand Authority Assessment Across 14 Signals**

We scan the broader web to evaluate your brand's digital footprint. The system checks for Knowledge Panel existence, Wikipedia presence (even mentions, not just dedicated pages), industry directory listings across 200+ authoritative sites, podcast appearances, guest articles, and social proof signals.

Citation pattern analysis reveals where authoritative sources reference your brand, products, or executives. We track mention frequency, context quality, and citation diversity—whether you're cited by a few sources repeatedly or many sources occasionally.

**Step 4: Structured Data Evaluation of 10 Implementation Factors**

Technical analysis examines your schema markup implementation. We identify which schema types you've deployed (Organization, Product, FAQ, HowTo, Article, etc.) and assess markup quality—whether it's properly formatted, contains required properties, and communicates clear meaning.

FAQ schema coverage measures how many of your question-answer sections include proper markup. Entity markup evaluation checks whether you've identified key concepts, people, products, and organizations using appropriate schema. Breadcrumb implementation affects how AI systems understand your content hierarchy.

**Step 5: Competitive Benchmarking Against Top 3 Competitors**

The system automatically identifies or accepts your input for your top three competitors, then runs identical analysis on their domains. You receive side-by-side scoring showing exactly where competitors outperform you across all 47 factors.

One client discovered their competitor appeared in 89% of Perplexity answers for category questions versus their 3%. The competitive analysis revealed the gap: their competitor had implemented FAQ schema on 127 pages, while they had zero FAQ markup. This single insight drove their entire optimization roadmap.

**Step 6: AI Citation Tracking Across 18 Platforms**

Our LLM Citation Tracker queries major AI platforms (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and 14 others) with industry-relevant questions to establish your current citation baseline. We measure mention frequency, citation context, and competitive share of voice.

This baseline becomes your "before" metric for measuring post-optimization improvements.

**Step 7: Roadmap Generation with Prioritized Recommendations**

The final analysis phase generates your 90-day roadmap. Rather than generic recommendations like "improve content quality," you receive specific, actionable items: "Add FAQ schema to these 23 existing pages," "Create answer-format content targeting these 47 questions," "Build citations on these 12 authoritative directories."

Each recommendation includes estimated impact scores (how much it will improve your AEO score) and implementation difficulty ratings (time and resources required). The prioritization framework focuses first on high-impact, low-difficulty improvements—the quick wins that build momentum.
