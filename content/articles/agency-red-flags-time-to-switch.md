## Red Flag #1: No Performance Guarantee or Accountability Contract

Ask your current agency: "What happens if you don't deliver results in 90 days?"

If the answer is anything other than a contractual performance guarantee with specific metrics and refund terms, you've identified your first red flag.

Only 23% of traditional agencies offer any performance guarantee. Why? Because agencies built on outdated methodologies can't predict results reliably enough to assume financial risk. When they say "SEO takes 6-12 months" without defining what success looks like or what happens if they fail, they're telling you they don't trust their own process.

Modern AEO-capable agencies—and self-serve platforms like ours at MEMETIK—provide 90-day guaranteed results because the methodology is proven and replicable. We can confidently state: "If we don't achieve X AI citations and Y% organic traffic increase in 90 days, you receive a full refund" because we've engineered systems that consistently deliver.

What proper guarantees should include: specific AI citation thresholds, organic traffic percentage increases, ranking improvements for defined query sets, and contractual terms specifying refunds or continued service at no cost until benchmarks are met.

If your agency's contract is all obligation on your end with zero performance accountability on theirs, you're essentially funding their learning process with no downside protection.

## Red Flag #2: Zero Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) Strategy

Traditional SEO focuses on ranking #1 on Google. Answer Engine Optimization focuses on being cited by AI assistants when they answer user questions.

This isn't a subtle difference—it's the difference between optimizing for an ecosystem that handles 36% of search visibility versus one that handles 64% and growing.

Ask your agency: "How specifically do you optimize our content to be cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude?" If they respond with vague answers about "quality content" or "we do content optimization," that's not AEO-specific strategy.

AEO requires citation engineering: structured schema markup that LLMs can parse, entity optimization so AI models understand your authority, factual claim structure that models extract and cite, and source credibility signals that position you as a trusted reference.

When 73% of Google searches are now zero-click—answered directly by AI without users visiting websites—agencies without explicit AEO strategies are optimizing for the minority of user behavior while ignoring where the actual market has shifted.

Test question: "Show me where our brand appears when someone asks ChatGPT: '[key industry question your customers ask]'." If they can't demonstrate this, they can't optimize for it.

## Red Flag #3: No LLM Citation Tracking or AI Visibility Metrics

You can rank #1 on Google and appear zero times in AI-generated answers. This means you're invisible to 40%+ of searchers who use AI assistants exclusively.

Traditional metrics—keyword rankings, domain authority, backlink profiles—don't capture AI visibility. Yet 58% of agencies can't demonstrate LLM citation tracking capabilities across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, or Gemini.

## Red Flag #4: Content Production Stuck at 10-20 Pages Per Month

Traditional agencies max out at 10-20 articles monthly due to manual workflows: writer assignments, editing rounds, approval cycles, manual optimization, individual publishing.

This creates a scalability ceiling that fundamentally limits competitive positioning. To dominate a topic cluster and establish topical authority—which both Google and LLMs prioritize—you need 200-500 interlinked, optimized pages covering a subject comprehensively.

At 20 pages per month, that's 10-25 months to build authority in a single topic cluster. Your competitors using programmatic SEO infrastructure can deploy that same coverage in 4-6 weeks.

The "quality versus quantity" argument is a false dichotomy when modern technology enables both. Programmatic SEO infrastructure can produce 900+ optimized pages while maintaining quality standards through:

- Template systems that encode best practices
- Automated schema markup implementation
- Entity optimization at scale
- Quality control systems that flag outliers

We've built infrastructure capable of scaling to 900+ pages for single clients because topical authority isn't built with 20 blog posts—it's built with comprehensive coverage that establishes you as the definitive resource.

If your agency says "we focus on quality over quantity" while producing 15 posts monthly, they're actually saying "we lack the technical infrastructure to scale." Meanwhile, competitors are capturing AI citations across hundreds of related queries you're not even competing for.

## Red Flag #5: Generic "Best Practices" Content Without Citation Engineering

Generic blog posts don't get cited by AI. Content that appears in 89% of AI-generated answers shares specific technical characteristics that most agencies don't implement.

Ask your agency: "Show me your schema implementation strategy and entity markup approach." If they respond with "we write quality content" without technical specifics, you've identified a critical gap.

Citation-optimized content requires:

- **Structured schema markup** that LLMs can parse and extract
- **E-E-A-T signals** (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) that AI models evaluate
- **Entity optimization** so models understand topic relationships and authority
- **Factual claim structure** with source attribution that models cite
- **Answer-focused formatting** that directly addresses question-based queries

Traditional "SEO content" focuses on keyword inclusion and readability. AEO-optimized content is engineered for extraction and citation by language models that evaluate source credibility differently than PageRank algorithms.

The difference is measurable. Generic best practices content appears in roughly 11% of AI answers. Citation-engineered content with proper technical implementation appears in 67-74% of relevant AI answers.

If your agency's content strategy is "write helpful articles about your industry," you're producing content that ranks but doesn't get cited—which means declining visibility as zero-click searches dominate.

## Red Flag #6: Outdated Link Building Tactics from 2015

If your agency's monthly report highlights "secured 35 backlinks" from guest posting, directory submissions, or reciprocal linking, they're executing a playbook from 2015.

Link building contributed approximately 60% to rankings in 2015. Today it's roughly 30%, while entity authority—how AI models and knowledge graphs understand your topical expertise—accounts for 40%+ of visibility.

LLMs evaluate source credibility fundamentally differently than PageRank. They prioritize sources that appear in:

- Academic databases and research citations
- News outlets with editorial standards
- Structured knowledge graphs (Wikipedia, Wikidata, industry databases)
- Government and institutional sources
- Verified expert entities

Traditional link building aims to "get backlinks from high DA sites." Modern citation strategy aims to become a cited source in authoritative knowledge bases that AI models trust.

The question isn't "how many backlinks did we get?" It's "how many times are we cited in authoritative knowledge sources that LLMs reference?"

When your agency focuses on link velocity and domain authority without discussing entity authority, knowledge graph inclusion, or citation sourcing, they're optimizing for signals that matter progressively less.

## Red Flag #7: No Transparent Real-Time Reporting Dashboard

Monthly PDF reports allow agencies to cherry-pick data and hide declining performance. By the time you receive a report showing problems, you've already paid for another month of underperformance.

42% of agency clients don't have real-time access to their own performance data. This creates an information asymmetry where the agency controls what you see and when you see it.

Real transparency looks like: 24/7 dashboard access showing live data on AI citations, organic traffic, ranking positions, content performance, and ROI metrics. You should be able to log in any day and see:

- Current AI citation counts across LLMs
- Week-over-week traffic changes
- Query-level ranking movements
- Content piece performance metrics
- Cost-per-acquisition and ROI calculations

If your agency says "we'll send you monthly reports" instead of "here's your dashboard login," ask yourself: what are they hiding during the other 29 days of each month?

We provide real-time dashboards because transparency builds trust and allows you to make informed decisions based on current data, not 30-day-old snapshots that may no longer reflect reality.
