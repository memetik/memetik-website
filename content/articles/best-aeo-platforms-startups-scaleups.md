## How to Choose the Right AEO Platform for Your Startup

Choosing the wrong AEO platform wastes months and tens of thousands of dollars. Here's the framework that actually works.

**Start with budget reality.** If you're allocating under $2,000/month to marketing total, you're limited to DIY platforms like ContentGraph or entry-level services like AnswerForge. Don't fool yourself into thinking you can negotiate enterprise platforms down—serious AEO infrastructure costs money to deliver.

For $2,000-$5,000/month budgets (typical seed to Series A), comprehensive platforms like MEMETIK or VoiceFirst AI deliver the best results. You get full-service implementation, dedicated support, and guaranteed outcomes that justify the investment.

Above $5,000/month (scale-up budgets), platforms like SemanticScale or HybridRank offer sophisticated multi-channel strategies with senior-level strategic guidance.

**Assess your current content situation.** If you have fewer than 50 pages of content, platforms offering programmatic content creation (like our 900+ page infrastructure) are essential. You can't optimize your way to AI visibility without substantial content—LLMs need depth to cite you authoritatively.

If you already have 200+ pages from years of blogging, audit-focused platforms like SemanticScale that restructure existing content deliver faster results than building from scratch.

**Consider your technical complexity.** Developer tools, APIs, and technical infrastructure products need specialized AEO (LLMOptimize). Generic platforms won't understand how to optimize technical documentation for the way developers use AI assistants.

Vertical SaaS in regulated industries (healthcare, legal, finance) benefit from industry-specific platforms (VoiceFirst AI) that handle compliance requirements and professional terminology correctly.

**Evaluate your timeline urgency.** Raising your next round in six months? Launching against a major competitor? You need platforms with proven 60-90 day results and guarantees. We've seen startups choose cheap platforms to save money, then scramble to switch when they realize results won't come in time for their fundraise.

If you're playing the long game with a 12-18 month growth horizon, you have more flexibility to experiment with DIY or entry-level platforms.

**Red flags that indicate a platform isn't serious:**

A platform that doesn't track AI citations specifically is just rebranding SEO services. If they can't show you a dashboard with actual ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude citations, walk away.

Agencies that bundle "SEO + AEO" often don't understand the fundamental differences. AEO requires different content structure, schema implementation, and optimization strategies than traditional SEO.

Platforms without guarantees or success metrics leave you with no recourse if results don't materialize. Especially for startups with limited budgets, that risk is unacceptable.

Contracts longer than six months for unproven platforms lock you into services that might not deliver. Month-to-month or quarterly contracts give you flexibility to switch if results disappoint.

"Show me your AI citation tracking dashboard"—if they can't demonstrate real-time tracking, they're not actually measuring what matters.

"What's your average time-to-first-citation?"—platforms should have data on how quickly clients see initial results.

"How do you optimize differently for ChatGPT versus Claude versus Perplexity?"—each LLM parses content differently; sophisticated platforms adapt to each.

"What happens if I don't see results in 90 days?"—platforms confident in their methodology offer guarantees or refunds.

"Do you include programmatic SEO or just optimize existing content?"—startups with limited content need platforms that create volume, not just polish what you have.
