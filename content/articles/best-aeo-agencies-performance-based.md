## How to Choose a Performance-Based AEO Agency

The agency landscape is filled with firms claiming to offer "performance-based" models that turn out to be traditional retainers with vague promises attached. Here's how to identify legitimate performance commitments and structure agreements that actually protect your investment.

### 1. Demand Specific, Measurable KPIs in Writing

Real performance guarantees specify exact metrics with exact targets and exact timeframes: "Increase AI citations by 25+ across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude within 90 days" or "Achieve top-five rankings for these specific 15 keywords within six months."

Vague guarantees like "improve SEO performance" or "increase online visibility" are meaningless and unenforceable. If an agency won't commit to specific numbers in a written contract, they're selling hope, not performance.

**Red flag:** Agencies that say "we'll guarantee results but need to assess your situation first before defining what results means." Legitimate performance agencies have standardized metrics they track across all clients.

### 2. Verify the Refund Mechanism is Clear and Fair

A true performance guarantee includes explicit refund terms: what percentage you receive back if targets aren't met, how underperformance is calculated, and what the refund request process looks like.

We offer a straightforward 90-day full refund if you don't see measurable improvement in AI citations and organic visibility. No complicated formulas, no disputes about whether "improvement" happened. Either the metrics moved meaningfully or they didn't.

**Red flag:** Agencies with refund clauses buried in dense contract language or those requiring you to prove they didn't try hard enough. The refund should be outcome-based, not effort-based.

### 3. Evaluate Their AEO Focus Percentage

As AI engines capture more search volume, agencies still optimizing primarily for traditional Google rankings are building on a declining foundation. Ask directly: "What percentage of your methodology focuses on AI citation optimization versus traditional SEO?"

Agencies below 40% AEO focus are fundamentally traditional SEO shops adding AI buzzwords to their marketing. We're 100% AEO-first because we built our entire infrastructure around answer engine visibility from the ground up.

**Red flag:** Agencies that can't clearly articulate how they optimize differently for ChatGPT versus Google, or those that treat "AI Overview optimization" as the same as traditional featured snippet optimization.

### 4. Assess Their Measurement Capabilities

You can't guarantee what you can't measure. Ask agencies to demonstrate their tracking capabilities: "Show me how you monitor AI citations across different platforms" and "What does your dashboard reporting look like?"

Our LLM visibility engineering platform tracks citations across 15+ AI platforms with brand mention frequency, context analysis, and competitive citation share. Traditional SEO tools can't do this because they weren't built for LLM monitoring.

**Red flag:** Agencies relying solely on traditional SEO tools (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz) without proprietary LLM tracking capabilities. These tools are excellent for traditional SEO but blind to AI engine citations.

### 5. Understand the Timeline and Why It Matters

Performance guarantee timelines reveal a lot about an agency's confidence and methodology. We offer 90-day guarantees because our AEO-first approach delivers measurable results within that timeframe. Agencies requiring 9-12 month commitments before guarantees kick in are hedging because their methods produce slower results.

Faster guarantees also reduce your risk exposure. If something isn't working, you learn within 90 days rather than burning a year of budget before discovering the strategy failed.

**Red flag:** Agencies requiring 6+ month commitments before performance clauses activate, especially those using "SEO takes time" as justification. AEO-optimized content gets cited by AI engines within weeks, not quarters.

### Questions to Ask During Agency Vetting

Before signing with any performance-based agency, get clear answers to these five questions:

1. **"What specific metrics do you guarantee improvement on, and by how much?"** — Demand numerical targets, not directional promises.

2. **"What happens if you don't hit targets—partial refund or full?"** — Understand exactly how underperformance is handled financially.

3. **"How do you track AI engine visibility beyond Google?"** — Verify they have actual LLM monitoring capabilities, not just traditional SEO tools.

4. **"What's your average time-to-results for companies like mine?"** — Get realistic timeline expectations based on their track record with similar clients.

5. **"Can I see a sample performance agreement with KPIs?"** — Review actual contract language before engaging in detailed discussions.

Agencies hesitant to answer these questions directly are selling traditional retainers dressed up as performance models.

## Why AEO Matters More Than Traditional SEO for Performance Guarantees

Answer Engine Optimization fundamentally changes the performance guarantee equation because it provides clearer attribution, more stable ranking logic, and faster measurable results than traditional SEO.

**The measurement advantage:** Traditional SEO operates in gray areas. Did you rank #5 because of the agency's work or because a competitor stopped publishing? Did traffic increase because of optimization or seasonal trends? These attribution challenges make traditional SEO guarantees difficult to enforce.

AEO metrics are more binary. Either ChatGPT cited your brand when answering a relevant query or it didn't. Either Perplexity included you in its answer set or it didn't. This clarity makes performance guarantees more enforceable and disputes less common.

Our LLM visibility engineering platform tracks your brand across 15+ AI platforms, measuring citation frequency with the same precision you'd track conversions in your CRM. When we guarantee 25+ AI citations within 90 days, both parties can objectively verify whether that happened.

**Algorithm stability:** Google makes over 500 algorithm updates annually, any of which can tank your rankings overnight regardless of how good your SEO is. This volatility makes guarantees risky for agencies and creates loopholes ("the algorithm changed, not our fault").

LLM citation logic is more stable because it's based on semantic relevance and content comprehensiveness rather than constantly shifting ranking factors. While AI models do get updated, the core principles of what makes content citation-worthy remain consistent.

This stability allows us to offer guarantees with confidence. When we deploy our 900+ page programmatic infrastructure optimized for LLM extraction, we know with high confidence it will generate citations because the methodology is proven across 50+ implementations.

**The future-proofing argument:** Guaranteeing traditional SEO results in 2024 is like guaranteeing Yellow Pages ad performance in 2010—technically possible but strategically misguided. With 58% of searches now beginning with AI engines, optimizing primarily for Google's ten blue links is building on a shrinking foundation.

Performance-based agencies should guarantee results in channels that matter for your future growth, not just your current state. If your agency can't demonstrate how they'll capture visibility in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude, they're guaranteeing performance in yesterday's medium.

**Programmatic scale creates predictability:** Traditional SEO agencies publish 4-8 blog posts monthly and hope for cumulative results. This low-volume approach makes performance unpredictable—maybe those eight articles gain traction, maybe they don't.

Our programmatic approach deploys 900+ optimized pages as a comprehensive content ecosystem. This scale creates statistical predictability. Not every page becomes a top citation source, but across hundreds of pages, citation frequency becomes mathematically predictable.

It's the difference between guaranteeing one sales rep will close deals versus guaranteeing a team of 100 reps will hit quota. Scale reduces variance and makes performance guarantees safer for both parties.

**The accountability unlock:** When outcomes are clearly measurable and causally linked to agency actions, accountability becomes straightforward. We can guarantee results because we know our infrastructure generates those results.

Agencies avoiding performance guarantees typically do so because their methodologies don't reliably produce measurable outcomes. They're experimenting with your budget rather than deploying proven systems.
