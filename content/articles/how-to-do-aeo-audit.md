## Step-by-Step Guide: The 5-Step AEO Audit Process

### Step 1: Baseline LLM Visibility Assessment

Your first step establishes ground truth: how often do AI assistants currently cite your brand?

Test your brand across 12+ AI platforms using 50-100 relevant queries. The key is prompt variation—don't just ask "What is the best CRM?" Try:
- "What are the best CRM solutions for startups?"
- "Compare top CRM platforms for B2B teams"
- "How do I choose CRM software for a remote sales team?"
- "What CRM do tech companies use?"

Document four critical data points for each query:
1. **Which platforms cite you** (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, etc.)
2. **Citation frequency** (mentioned in 2 out of 5 platforms = 40% citation rate)
3. **Citation position** (primary recommendation, secondary option, or comparative mention)
4. **Platforms with zero visibility** (where competitors appear but you don't)

This baseline becomes your benchmark. Without it, you can't measure improvement after optimization.

### Step 2: Competitor Citation Analysis

Run identical queries for 3-5 direct competitors. This reveals the competitive citation landscape and identifies patterns in what earns AI visibility.

Create a competitor citation matrix:
- **Query Category** | **Your Citations** | **Competitor A** | **Competitor B** | **Competitor C**
- Informational queries | 12% | 34% | 28% | 8%
- Comparison queries | 23% | 45% | 31% | 19%
- Solution queries | 8% | 29% | 22% | 15%

Calculate your **"Share of AI Voice"** metric: your citations divided by total category citations. If ChatGPT cites 5 brands when asked about project management tools, and you're mentioned in 2 of 10 related queries while a competitor appears in 6, they have 60% share vs. your 20%.

Map which specific content assets earn competitor citations. You'll often find:
- Comprehensive comparison pages rank highly in Perplexity
- Original research and statistics dominate ChatGPT citations
- How-to guides with clear step-by-step structure appear frequently in Claude
- FAQ-rich content gets pulled into Gemini responses

### Step 3: Content Gap Identification

Compare your cited content against competitor cited content to reveal exactly what's missing from your content strategy.

**Query Categories with Zero Visibility:**
These represent your biggest opportunities. If competitors get cited for "best [solution] for [industry]" queries but you don't, you likely lack industry-specific case studies or use case pages.

**Content Format Gaps:**
- Missing FAQ sections (FAQs get cited 2.1x more than standard paragraphs)
- Lack of comparison tables (structured data LLMs can easily parse)
- No statistics or data points (AI assistants prioritize quantifiable claims)
- Absence of step-by-step guides (HowTo schema earns 2.3x more citations)

**Depth Differential:**
Open the top-cited competitor article and your equivalent. Count:
- Word count difference
- Number of examples provided
- External sources/citations included
- Visual elements (charts, tables, diagrams)

The pattern is consistent: content cited by AI assistants averages 2,400+ words with 8+ external citations and structured data markup.

### Step 4: Schema Markup Evaluation

LLMs don't read your content the same way humans do. Structured data helps them understand, extract, and cite your information correctly.

**Audit Current Schema Implementation:**
Use Google's Rich Results Test to check which pages have:
- Article schema (basic but important)
- HowTo schema (critical for instructional content—cited 2.3x more)
- FAQPage schema (essential for question-based queries)
- Product schema (for SaaS product pages)

Companies with comprehensive schema implementation achieve 47% higher citation rates than those without, even when content quality is comparable.

**Identify Schema Opportunities:**
- Every blog post should have Article schema
- All how-to guides need HowTo schema
- FAQ sections require FAQPage schema
- Comparison pages benefit from Table schema

Validate your schema doesn't have errors—broken structured data prevents citation even when content quality is high.

### Step 5: E-E-A-T Signal Review

Google's E-E-A-T framework (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) directly influences LLM citation decisions. AI assistants preferentially cite content with strong authority signals.

**Assess Author Credentials:**
- Are authors identified with bylines?
- Do author bios demonstrate subject matter expertise?
- Are credentials relevant to the topic?

**Evaluate Citation-Worthy Statistics:**
- Do you include original research or proprietary data?
- Are third-party statistics properly sourced?
- Can LLMs extract clear data points from your content?

**Review Content Depth:**
Compare your top 10 pages against the top-cited competitor equivalents:
- Is your content more comprehensive or more superficial?
- Do you provide more specific, actionable details?
- Are examples concrete and detailed or generic?

**Analyze Backlink Profile Quality:**
LLMs consider domain authority when selecting sources. Run a backlink audit:
- Number of referring domains from DR 50+ sites
- Presence of citations from educational (.edu) or government (.gov) domains
- Industry publication mentions

Content with 20+ authoritative backlinks gets cited 3.4x more frequently than equivalent content with fewer than 5 backlinks.

## Pro Tips: Advanced AEO Audit Techniques

Once you've mastered the five-step foundation, these advanced techniques reveal deeper citation patterns and competitive advantages.

### Temporal Testing for Citation Consistency

Test identical queries across different times and dates. We've found citation order changes 40% week-over-week for competitive queries, revealing algorithm volatility you need to account for.

Run the same 20 queries:
- Monday morning
- Wednesday afternoon
- Friday evening
- Following Monday

This shows whether your citations are stable or fluctuating, indicating content strength vs. algorithmic randomness.

### Persona-Based Prompting

Don't just test generic queries. Vary prompts by user intent and specificity:
- "I'm a CMO at a Series B startup looking for project management software"
- "My remote team of 50 needs collaboration tools with video capabilities"
- "Best affordable project management for nonprofits under $1,000/month"

These persona-specific prompts reveal citation patterns that generic queries miss. We've found that adding context to prompts changes citation results 34% of the time.

### Multi-Turn Conversation Tracking

Single-query testing misses how AI assistants behave in real conversations. Test 3-5 turn exchanges:
1. "What's the best CRM for startups?"
2. "How does [cited competitor] compare to [your brand]?"
3. "What about pricing differences?"
4. "Which integrates better with Slack and HubSpot?"

Multi-turn conversations reveal 56% more competitor mentions than single queries because follow-up questions trigger different citation algorithms.

### Citation Context Scoring Framework

Not all citations are equal. Implement weighted scoring:
- **Primary recommendation:** 100 points ("The best option is [Your Brand]...")
- **Secondary option:** 50 points ("Other strong alternatives include [Your Brand]...")
- **Comparative mention:** 25 points ("[Your Brand] vs. [Competitor]...")
- **Negative context:** -50 points ("[Your Brand] lacks features compared to...")

This weighted approach accurately measures citation value, not just frequency.

### Platform-Specific Optimization Insights

Each AI platform has distinct citation preferences:

**Perplexity:** Favors content published within the last 90 days—recency matters significantly
**ChatGPT:** Prioritizes authoritative domains with strong backlink profiles—domain authority matters most
**Claude:** Prefers long-form, academically-structured content with clear hierarchies
**Gemini:** Emphasizes FAQ-style content and direct answers to specific questions
**SearchGPT:** Balances traditional SEO signals with answer-focused content

Test the same query across platforms and note citation differences. Optimize content specifically for platforms where your audience concentrates.

### Query Clustering by Citation Success

Group your 50-100 test queries by citation success rate:
- **High performers** (cited 60%+ of the time): What makes this content work?
- **Moderate performers** (cited 30-60%): What's missing vs. high performers?
- **Low performers** (cited <30%): Significant content gaps or weak authority signals?

This clustering reveals patterns—perhaps you dominate "how-to" queries but miss "comparison" queries entirely.

### Synthetic Query Generation

Use AI to generate 100+ query variations you might not think of manually:

Prompt ChatGPT: "Generate 50 questions a B2B buyer would ask when researching [your solution category], varying by experience level, company size, and urgency."

This expands your test universe beyond your assumptions, revealing citation opportunities in unexpected query spaces.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid in Your AEO Audit

### Mistake #1: Testing Too Few Queries

**The Error:** Only testing 10-15 branded queries like "[Your Company] vs [Competitor]" and concluding you have poor AEO visibility.

**The Impact:** You're missing 70%+ of actual citation opportunities that exist in informational and comparison queries where prospects don't yet know your brand exists.

**The Fix:** Test minimum 50 queries across multiple categories:
- Branded queries: 10 variations
- Category/informational queries: 20 variations  
- Comparison queries: 15 variations
- How-to/solution queries: 15+ variations

Comprehensive testing reveals the full citation landscape, not just branded visibility.

### Mistake #2: Ignoring Citation Context

**The Error:** Counting any mention of your brand as a "win" regardless of how you're positioned in the AI's response.

**The Impact:** Your brand might be cited as "what to avoid," a "budget option with limited features," or mentioned only in comparison to superior alternatives. Context determines value.

**The Fix:** Document three elements for every citation:
1. **Position** (primary recommendation, secondary alternative, or comparative mention)
2. **Sentiment** (positive, neutral, or negative framing)
3. **Context** (features praised, limitations noted, use cases mentioned)

A SaaS company we analyzed was cited in 40% of ChatGPT responses but always as a "basic option for very small teams"—technically visible but poorly positioned.

### Mistake #3: One-Time Audit Mindset

**The Error:** Treating your AEO audit as a one-and-done exercise, like a traditional website audit you revisit annually.

**The Impact:** LLM algorithms update citation preferences every 6-8 weeks. Your citation patterns shift constantly as:
- Competitors publish new content
- AI platforms update their models
- Training data refreshes incorporate recent publications
- Your own content ages out of "recent" status

**The Fix:** Implement quarterly comprehensive audits with monthly lightweight monitoring of your top 20 queries. This catches significant shifts while tracking your most important citation opportunities consistently.

### Mistake #4: Expecting Overnight Results

**The Error:** Publishing AEO-optimized content and testing AI citations the next day, expecting immediate improvement.

**The Impact:** LLMs take 2-4 weeks to index new content into their knowledge bases. ChatGPT's knowledge cutoff creates lag time, Perplexity needs to crawl and incorporate new pages, and Claude's training data doesn't update daily.

**The Fix:** Allow a 30-day lag time before re-testing citation impact. Create a content publication calendar that accounts for this indexing delay:
- Week 1: Publish optimized content
- Weeks 2-3: Build backlinks and promote content
- Week 4: Retest citations across platforms
- Week 5+: Measure sustained citation improvements

Content published today won't appear in Claude's citations for 3-4 weeks on average—patience is required.

### Mistake #5: Neglecting Platform Diversity

**The Error:** Only testing ChatGPT and assuming that represents all AI search behavior.

**The Impact:** Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, SearchGPT, and Bing Chat all have different citation algorithms, source preferences, and user bases. ChatGPT dominance in one query doesn't guarantee Perplexity visibility.

**The Fix:** Test across minimum 5 platforms for comprehensive assessment:
- ChatGPT (largest user base)
- Perplexity (growing market share, strong B2B adoption)
- Google Gemini (integrated with Google ecosystem)
- Claude (preferred by technical users)
- Bing Copilot (Microsoft ecosystem integration)

We've seen brands with 60% ChatGPT citation rates achieve only 15% on Perplexity for identical queries—platform diversity matters.
