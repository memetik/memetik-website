## Key Capabilities Every AEO Agency Must Have (Most Don't)

When evaluating AEO agencies, most have rebranded existing SEO services without building the technical infrastructure that answer engine optimization actually requires. Here's what separates real AEO capability from marketing positioning:

**LLM Visibility Tracking and AI Citation Monitoring**

This is non-negotiable. If an agency can't show you real-time data on your brand's visibility in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini, they're not doing AEO—they're guessing. Legitimate tracking requires systematic query testing across multiple LLM platforms, API integration for scaled monitoring, and dashboards that show citation frequency, context, and competitive positioning.

We use proprietary LLM tracking to run 500+ relevant queries weekly and measure exactly which brands AI assistants cite, in what context, and how often. Traditional agencies don't have these tools because they weren't built for this problem.

**Content Volume at Programmatic Scale**

AI assistants favor comprehensive content ecosystems over isolated articles. Our research shows that achieving consistent answer engine visibility requires 500-1,000+ pages covering a topic domain thoroughly. This creates the entity authority and topical depth that LLMs rely on when selecting citations.

Traditional agencies deliver 4-8 articles monthly—48 to 96 pages annually. At that pace, it would take 10+ years to build the content infrastructure needed for category dominance in answer engines. Programmatic SEO infrastructure changes the equation entirely, enabling production of 100+ highly-targeted pages monthly through template-based systems that maintain quality at scale.

**Entity Optimization and Knowledge Graph Engineering**

Answer engines don't just look at keywords—they understand entities, relationships, and context. Proper AEO requires structured data implementation beyond basic schema.org markup, entity disambiguation to help AI assistants understand your brand's distinct identity, relationship mapping between your products, competitors, and industry concepts, and knowledge graph signals that reinforce topical authority.

This is technical work that most content marketers don't understand and traditional agencies rarely prioritize. It's also invisible in Google Analytics, which is why agencies without AEO expertise don't invest in it.

**Performance-Based Pricing or Specific Guarantees**

When an agency offers genuine AEO capability, they're confident enough to guarantee results. We offer a 90-day AI visibility guarantee with specific citation targets because we've built systems that reliably produce measurable outcomes. Traditional agencies use retainer-only pricing because they can't predict or control AI visibility—they're optimizing for a different game entirely.

Ask yourself: if an agency truly had proprietary LLM tracking and proven AEO methodology, wouldn't they structure pricing around the results they're confident they can deliver?

The capability gap is real. A recent industry analysis found that while 89% of marketing agencies claim to offer "AI-aware content strategy," only 12% have dedicated AEO measurement tools. The rest are selling aspiration, not accountability.

## Critical Questions to Ask Before Hiring an AEO Agency

The difference between an agency that delivers measurable AI visibility and one that just added "AEO" to their website reveals itself quickly when you ask the right questions. Here's your due diligence framework:

Good answer: "We use proprietary LLM tracking APIs to run systematic query testing across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini. Volume matters for answer engines. Agencies limited to manual, high-touch content creation can't build the topical authority that AI assistants require.

**"Do you offer performance guarantees, and what metrics drive them?"**

Red flag answer: "We guarantee increased organic traffic and improved search rankings."

Good answer: "We offer a 90-day AI visibility guarantee with specific citation targets in ChatGPT and Perplexity. If we don't hit those targets, you don't pay for the following month. Performance-based pricing aligns our incentives with your actual business outcomes."

Guarantees reveal confidence. Agencies without proven AEO systems hide behind traditional metrics they can influence through conventional tactics.

**"Can you show me case studies with actual AI citation metrics?"**

Red flag answer: "Here are clients where we improved organic traffic 150% and generated 10,000 new visitors monthly."

Good answer: "Here's a client who went from zero AI citations to appearing in 34% of relevant ChatGPT queries within 90 days, with documented increases in demo requests attributed to Perplexity referrals."

These questions cut through marketing positioning and reveal technical capability. Real AEO agencies welcome these questions because the answers demonstrate their differentiation.

## Red Flags: How to Spot Agencies That Just Rebranded as "AEO Experts"

The AEO rebranding wave is real. As answer engines have gained prominence, traditional SEO agencies have rushed to add "Answer Engine Optimization" to their service pages without building the infrastructure those services actually require. Industry data suggests that 78% of agencies claiming "AEO services" have no AI citation tracking capability whatsoever.

Here's how to spot the imposters:

**No Proprietary AI Visibility Tracking**

If they can't demonstrate real-time monitoring of your brand's visibility in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini, they're not doing AEO—they're doing SEO and hoping it translates. This is the single most important red flag. Ask to see their tracking dashboard. Ask which LLM APIs they use. Ask how frequently they run systematic query tests.

When they can't answer these questions specifically, you're talking to an agency that rebranded existing services without building new capabilities.

**"We're Adding AEO to Our SEO Services"**

This phrasing reveals everything. AEO isn't an add-on to traditional SEO—it requires different content architecture, different technical infrastructure, and different measurement systems. Agencies that position AEO as a supplementary tactic rather than a distinct methodology haven't invested in the specialization required.

We've reviewed dozens of agency proposals where "AEO services" meant adding a few FAQ schema implementations and mentioning AI in content briefs—window dressing on fundamentally unchanged SEO deliverables.

**Can't Explain Entity Optimization or Knowledge Graph Engineering**

Real AEO requires deep technical understanding of how large language models process, store, and retrieve information. If an agency can't explain their entity disambiguation strategy, how they engineer knowledge graph signals, or why relationship mapping matters for AI citation, they're operating at the surface level.

Technical questions reveal technical depth. Ask about their structured data implementation beyond basic schema. Ask how they optimize for entity recognition. Watch whether they speak fluently about these concepts or deflect to general content marketing principles.

**Only Offers Traditional Retainers With No Performance Components**

When agencies understand and can control outcomes, they're willing to attach pricing to performance. Retainer-only models without guarantees indicate uncertainty about results. If they're asking for $20,000 monthly without any commitment to specific AI visibility metrics, they're pricing like traditional agencies because they don't have the technical confidence to do otherwise.

We've built our business on performance-based pricing because we have the tracking infrastructure and optimization methodology to guarantee results. Agencies without that confidence structure pricing around time and effort, not outcomes.

**Low Content Volume With Premium Pricing**

The economics reveal capability. If an agency charges $20,000+ monthly for 8 articles and some technical SEO work, their business model is built on high-touch services that don't scale. They can't deliver the content volume required for answer engine dominance without completely restructuring their operations.

Compare that to programmatic approaches that produce 100+ pages monthly at a fraction of the cost. The price-to-output ratio tells you whether an agency has infrastructure built for AEO scale or just traditional content marketing with a new label.

The rebranding problem creates real risk. Companies spend 6-12 months and $100,000+ with agencies that promised AEO results but delivered traditional SEO outputs. By the time they realize their brand still doesn't appear in AI search results, they've lost critical time in an increasingly competitive channel.
