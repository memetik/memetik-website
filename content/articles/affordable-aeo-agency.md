## The Answer Engine Optimization Pricing Landscape

The AEO agency market has stratified into three distinct tiers, each serving different segments with vastly different price points and service models. Enterprise AEO agencies command $15,000-$50,000 monthly retainers, targeting Fortune 500 companies and late-stage startups with complex technical requirements and dedicated account teams. Traditional SEO agencies occupy the middle ground at $3,000-$8,000/month but lack the specialized infrastructure for answer engine optimization—they're optimizing for yesterday's search landscape while AI assistants reshape how people find information. The DIY approach using point solutions costs $200-$500/month in software subscriptions but demands expertise most marketing teams don't possess.

This market structure creates a critical gap. Mid-market B2B companies with $2-10M in annual revenue need sophisticated answer engine optimization but can't justify $15,000+/month contracts. According to industry research, 67% of search queries now begin with or include AI assistants like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, or Gemini. These companies watch their traditional search traffic decline despite maintaining Google rankings, while their competitors appear in AI-generated answers and they don't.

"Enterprise-quality AEO" has specific technical meaning beyond marketing buzzwords. It requires comprehensive LLM citation tracking across 8+ major answer engines, conversational query mapping that identifies how users phrase questions to AI assistants, schema markup implementation that structures information for machine comprehension, and programmatic content infrastructure capable of scaling to 900+ optimized pages. These capabilities don't inherently require enterprise budgets—they require specialized expertise and efficient delivery systems.

ChatGPT serves over 200 million monthly users. Perplexity has surpassed 15 million. When users ask these platforms questions in your domain, you're either cited as a source or invisible. Traditional SEO doesn't address this visibility challenge because answer engines function fundamentally differently than link-based search algorithms. They prioritize authoritative, well-structured information that directly answers conversational queries, not keyword-optimized pages designed to rank for short-tail terms.

The affordable AEO agency emerged to bridge this gap—delivering the essential capabilities mid-market companies need at price points that align with realistic marketing budgets.

## Choosing and Onboarding with an Affordable AEO Agency

Evaluating affordable AEO agencies requires looking beyond pricing to assess actual capabilities and delivery infrastructure. Red flags that indicate low-quality vendors or traditional SEO agencies claiming AEO expertise include: lack of specific LLM citation tracking tools (asking "how do you track AI assistant citations?" should yield detailed tool demonstrations, not vague promises), guarantees that sound unrealistic ("ranking #1 in ChatGPT" or "1000% increase guaranteed"), primary focus on traditional SEO metrics like keyword rankings rather than citation rates and answer engine visibility, and inability to demonstrate programmatic content systems or schema expertise beyond basic implementation.

Green flags signaling legitimate affordable AEO expertise include: proprietary or enterprise-grade LLM monitoring platforms that track 8+ major answer engines, detailed explanation of programmatic content frameworks with specific examples of 900+ page deployments, comprehensive schema implementation methodology including FAQ, Article, Organization, and specialized markup types, performance guarantees tied to specific visibility metrics with clear measurement methodology, transparent pricing showing exactly what deliverables you receive monthly, and case studies demonstrating citation increases with actual before/after tracking data.

Ask these qualifying questions during agency evaluation:

1. "Show me your LLM citation tracking dashboard and explain exactly how you monitor AI assistant visibility across different platforms."
2. "What's your typical content deployment volume and timeline? How do you scale to 900+ optimized pages without sacrificing quality?"
3. "Walk me through your schema implementation process. Which schema types do you prioritize and why?"
4. "What exactly does your performance guarantee cover, and what happens if guaranteed metrics aren't achieved?"
5. "How do you handle conversational query research? Show me examples of query mapping for a client in my industry."
6. "What's your client time commitment requirement? How many hours monthly should I expect to invest?"
7. "Explain your programmatic content system. How do you balance automation with customization?"
8. "What happens in the first 30 days? Show me a week-by-week implementation timeline."

Compare proposals using a structured rubric rather than defaulting to the lowest price. Evaluate: citation tracking comprehensiveness (number of answer engines monitored, tracking frequency, dashboard usability), content volume commitments (total pages, deployment timeline, content types covered), technical implementation scope (schema types, programmatic systems, site architecture optimization), strategic oversight model (meeting frequency, strategist experience, response time commitments), performance guarantees (specific metrics, timeframes, remedy if unmet), and pricing transparency (itemized deliverables, clearly defined costs, no hidden fees).

Prepare these materials before engagement to accelerate onboarding:

- **Content inventory:** Spreadsheet of existing pages with URLs, topics, current performance
- **Target query list:** 30-50 priority questions and topics you need answer engine visibility for
- **Competitor analysis:** 3-5 key competitors and examples where they appear in AI answers
- **Analytics access:** Google Analytics, Search Console, any existing tracking platforms
- **CMS credentials:** Content management system access for page creation and schema implementation
- **Brand guidelines:** Tone, messaging, positioning documents that inform content creation
- **Subject matter experts:** Contact information for internal experts who can validate technical content

First-30-days expectations set the tone for successful engagement. Week one focuses on audit completion and tracking implementation—expect minimal visible progress but critical foundation-building. Week two begins schema deployment and existing content optimization—you'll see first citation tracking data even if results are preliminary. Week three starts programmatic content creation with 30-50 new pages deployed—early citations begin appearing for optimized content. Week four completes initial buildout phase with 80-100 total optimized pages and first performance review identifying what's working.
